# AGENTS.md — Axodus Institutional Publications

## Mission

Maintain a controlled research repository for academically legible,
traceable, and non-promotional Axodus publications.

## Required workflow

1. Read `governance/evidence-boundaries.md` before drafting public text.
2. Register material claims in `research/claims-register.md`.
3. Verify bibliographic metadata before moving a source from candidates to the
   verified bibliography or `references.bib`.
4. Keep Markdown and LaTeX versions of a manuscript structurally equivalent.
5. Apply `governance/publication-review-checklist.md`.
6. Record blockers and review status in the manuscript's readiness report.

## Non-negotiable rules

- Do not invent references, authors, identifiers, URLs, data, metrics,
  implementations, users, results, guarantees, or adoption.
- Distinguish evidence-supported facts, internal claims, hypotheses, design
  proposals, and future work.
- Use `[REFERENCE NEEDED]` when a scholarly assertion lacks support.
- Do not imply operational maturity.
- Do not include source code, credentials, private repository names,
  infrastructure configuration, secrets, or sensitive intellectual property.
- Do not use fundraising language, token-price speculation, investment
  promises, or promotional superlatives.
- Do not publish, submit, contact external parties, commit, push, or open a
  pull request unless a human explicitly authorizes that separate action.

## Axodus-specific boundaries

- Treat precise duration, number of cores, and core maturity statuses as
  blocked internal claims.
- The only currently permitted historical formulation is: “The project
  originates from a multi-year independent research and design effort.”
- L0–L5 and D0–D5 are proposed maturity axes. Do not infer level definitions.
- “Proof of Knowledge” means a proposed educational participation mechanism
  in Axodus documents. It is not a cryptographic proof-of-knowledge protocol.

## Writing style

Use precise academic English for manuscripts. Prefer bounded claims and
testable propositions. Describe Axodus as a conceptual, prototype-stage model.
Avoid “revolutionary,” “unique,” “first,” “guaranteed,” “disruptive,” and
similar language unless independently established and necessary.

## Manuscript structure

Use, as applicable: abstract, keywords, introduction, related work, problem
statement, conceptual model, governance, evaluation plan, limitations,
research agenda, conclusion, and references.

## Verification

At minimum, check citation/BibTeX consistency, unsupported claims,
promotional language, maturity ambiguity, sensitive disclosures, and
correspondence metadata. A draft is not ready for publication merely because
these checks pass.
