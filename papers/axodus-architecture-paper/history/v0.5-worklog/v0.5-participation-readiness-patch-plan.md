## Current Role of Section 5

Section `5` currently presents participation readiness as a supporting
institutional interface for learning, assessment, bounded eligibility, and
appeal in selected consequential contexts. Its intended role is supportive,
not central. It is meant to frame one possible way of preparing or screening
participation in bounded governance contexts while preserving contestability
and proportionality concerns.

## Planned Modification Type

- controlled participation-readiness boundary refinement

## Allowed Intervention

Future Section `5` work may only:

- preserve the section as a supporting governance mechanism rather than a
  co-equal contribution;
- clarify that participation readiness is a bounded participation-support
  mechanism, not a standalone educational product;
- clarify that educational preparation, assessment, or credential-like
  artifacts do not by themselves establish legitimacy, fairness, or authority;
- emphasize proportionality, accommodations, and contestability concerns;
- keep the section conceptually tied to bounded governance participation
  rather than to product, platform, or program identity;
- preserve hypothesis language where empirical benefit is not established; and
- align the section with existing evidence, traceability, contestability, and
  prototype-stage boundaries.

## Forbidden Intervention

## Claim-Boundary Risks

The future Section `5` patch must treat the following as live risks:

- participation readiness can drift into educational-product framing;
- credential-like language can sound like a strong legitimacy claim;
- readiness requirements can sound exclusionary if proportionality is not
  preserved;
- educational framing can drift into internal Axodus branding;
- readiness mechanisms can be misread as tokenomics-adjacent if not clearly
  bounded away from incentives and distribution logic; and
- assessment language can imply validated fairness or authority when none is
  evidenced.

## Proposed Future Patch Shape

The future Section `5` patch should:

- keep the section compact;
- preserve its supporting role rather than expanding it;
- avoid new subsections unless strictly necessary;
- avoid new citations;
- avoid examples that imply a live educational product, user population,
  certification program, or operating curriculum;
- explicitly state that readiness artifacts do not by themselves establish
  legitimacy, authorization, or fairness;
- connect participation readiness to bounded governance participation,
  proportionality, accommodations, and challengeability; and
- avoid any tokenomics or internal-brand drift.
