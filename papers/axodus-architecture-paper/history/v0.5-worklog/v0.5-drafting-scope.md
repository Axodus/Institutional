## v0.5 Drafting Objectives

The v0.5 drafting scope remains centered on:

- traceability;
- accountability;
- contestability;
- human-AI-assisted digital institutions;
- bounded governance abstraction;
- institutional design under evidence boundaries;
- architecture-level explanation;
- reconstructability;
- procedural review; and
- AI assistance under human-governed constraints.

Within those bounds, v0.5 may improve:

- architectural clarity;
- section structure;
- conceptual precision;
- explanation of governance boundaries;
- the relationship between constitutional center and bounded local governance
  domains;
- the relationship among proposal, review, ratification, contestation, and
  escalation;
- AI supervision as bounded assistance rather than autonomous authority; and
- evaluation framing as synthetic, conceptual, or proposed only.

## Allowed Manuscript Interventions

If a later v0.5 drafting phase is opened, the following intervention types are
allowed within the scope of this document:

- title or abstract refinement that strengthens bounded governance framing
  without implying readiness or validation;
- introduction and problem-statement clarification that better defines the
  governance problem under constitutional constraints;
- architecture-section refinement that improves conceptual layering,
  reconstructability, reviewability, and bounded authority explanation;
- evaluation-plan tightening that keeps all measures and scenarios explicitly
  proposed, synthetic, or conceptual; and
- conclusion or research-agenda refinement that preserves prototype-stage
  and non-operational boundaries.

No intervention authorized by this scope document may convert conceptual
governance design into implementation evidence, operational description, or
publication-ready positioning.

## Evidence Boundaries

v0.5 drafting remains subject to `../../governance/evidence-boundaries.md`.
Key consequences for the drafting scope are:

- Axodus may be described only as a conceptual, prototype-stage model.
- Proposed evaluation may be described as future work, not as evidence.
- Governance, accountability, modularity, and human oversight remain design
  objectives and research topics rather than validated achievements.
- Statements allowed in Institutional documentation are not automatically
  allowed in the academic manuscript.

## Disclosure Boundaries

v0.5 drafting remains subject to
`../../governance/public-disclosure-boundary.md`. Key consequences are:

- public module disclosure does not authorize product cataloging in the paper;
- public governance-mechanics disclosure does not authorize governance
  implementation claims in the paper;
- financially sensitive, regulated, strategic, or implementation-specific
  material remains outside the drafting scope; and
- the academic paper must continue to abstract product inventory and focus on
  governance architecture, traceability, accountability, and human oversight.

## Claim Boundaries

v0.5 drafting remains subject to `../../research/claims-register.md`. The
current scope particularly depends on:

- AX-006 and AX-007 blocking operational readiness, users, adoption, and
  impact claims;
- AX-018, AX-020, AX-022, and AX-023 blocking implementation, provenance,
  and evaluation overclaims;
- AX-024, AX-026, AX-030, and AX-032 allowing bounded governance and module
  abstractions as design proposals only;
- AX-031 keeping Proof of Knowledge out of the manuscript body; and
- AX-033 and AX-034 limiting institutional governance labels and tokenomics
  disclosure.

## Terminology Risks

The following terms remain sensitive and must be handled cautiously in any
later v0.5 manuscript work:

- `Tenant`: institutionally valid, but paper-facing use should prefer
  `bounded local governance domain`
- `service nuclei`: should not drive manuscript-layer terminology where
  `functional service modules` is safer
- `Proof of Knowledge`: internal Axodus term only, excluded from manuscript
  body
- `Executive`, `Boardroom`, and `Community`: controlled institutional labels,
  not primary manuscript structure labels
- `federated`: institutional and conceptual descriptor, not a protocol claim
