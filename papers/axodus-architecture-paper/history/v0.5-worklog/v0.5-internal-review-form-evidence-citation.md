## Overall assessment

In 150–300 words, summarize the paper's evidence discipline, strongest
citation-control feature, principal claim risk, and most important revision:

Response:
The manuscript shows materially improved evidence discipline. It repeatedly
frames Axodus as a prototype-stage conceptual architecture, keeps tokenomics
and operational claims out of the paper body, and distinguishes design
proposals from validation claims with unusual consistency for a governance
paper of this type. Its strongest citation-control feature is the explicit
separation between verified bibliography records, evidence-boundary policy,
and manuscript-facing claims, which reduces pressure to overstate what the
citations establish. The principal claim risk is not a misused scholarly
source, but a version-control inconsistency: `paper.md` still carries front
matter that labels the manuscript `Draft v0.4` and `Ready for author review
of internal v0.4 draft`, while the package and readiness files correctly
treat the manuscript as `v0.5 internal draft`. A secondary risk is that the
Markdown front matter still contains `@`-style contact metadata that naive
citation parsers can misread as bibliography keys. The most important
revision is therefore a bounded readiness-and-metadata cleanup, not a new
bibliography pass.
