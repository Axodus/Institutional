## Current Review Risks Identified

The current repository state still presents the following pre-package review
risks:

### 1. Readiness-label ambiguity

Top-level status language is substantially cleaner after Phase 1.37, but the
repository still carries a layered readiness history. A future package phase
should confirm that:

- active `v0.5` status is clearly distinguished from historical `v0.4` and
  `v0.3.2` records; and
- `Ready for human review: yes` is not misread as authorization for internal
  peer review.

### 2. Citation-audit nuance

The scholarly citation sets in `paper.md`, `paper.tex`, and `references.bib`
are aligned, but raw Markdown `@...` parsing still captures contact-style
metadata strings. A future package phase must treat those as metadata, not as
orphan citation keys.

### 3. Residual sensitive-claim language

The manuscript still contains terms such as `deployed`, `validated`,
`operational`, `adoption`, and `production operation` in boundary-preserving,
taxonomy, or evaluation contexts. A future package phase should verify that:

- each retained use is justified by context;
- no retained use reads as operational maturity or validated performance; and
- any remaining ambiguous use is softened before the package is opened.

### 4. Cross-format consistency duty

The manuscript has been patched in many bounded phases. Before a package is
opened, `paper.md` and `paper.tex` need a dedicated pairwise consistency
review focused on:

- abstract;
- introduction;
- problem statement;
- conceptual architecture and Section 4 subsections;
- Section 5 participation readiness;
- Section 8 evaluation plan;
- limitations;
- research agenda; and
- conclusion.

## Planned v0.5 Author Review Checklist

The future package-opening phase should require the following author-side
checklist to pass before any internal reviewer receives the manuscript.

### A. Scope and status

- confirm the active manuscript line is `v0.5 internal draft`;
- confirm no peer-review package is already active;
- confirm no publication or external-circulation language has been introduced;
- confirm all Phase 1 evidence and disclosure blockers remain active.

### B. Sensitive-claim review

- confirm no tokenomics language appears in the manuscript body;
- confirm no named institutional agents appear in the manuscript body;
- confirm no product inventory or implementation inventory appears in the
  manuscript body beyond the bounded abstractions already approved;
- confirm no operational, adoption, user, production, financial, security, or
  authority claims exceed current evidence boundaries;
- confirm residual negative statements remain useful and not contradictory.

### C. Markdown/LaTeX consistency

- compare section headings across `paper.md` and `paper.tex`;
- compare abstract, introduction, problem statement, sections `4.1` through
  `4.6`, Section `5`, Section `8`, Section `9`, Section `10`, and Section
  `11` pairwise;
- confirm there is no Markdown-only conceptual drift;
- confirm there is no LaTeX-only conceptual drift;
- confirm any line-break or paragraph-structure differences are only
  formatting differences.

### D. Citation and bibliography audit

- confirm the effective scholarly citation keys match across Markdown and
  LaTeX;
- confirm `references.bib` still contains no orphan scholarly entries;
- confirm contact metadata is excluded from citation-key parsing;
- confirm no new citation has been inserted without a matching verified
  bibliography basis.

### E. Readiness and package discipline

- confirm current readiness language does not imply peer-review authorization;
- confirm the package remains internal-only;
- confirm reviewer instructions preserve confidentiality and
  non-distribution;
- confirm reviewer materials do not imply controlled external review,
  publication, or submission.

## Planned Reviewer Instruction Priorities

The future v0.5 reviewer instructions should tell reviewers to focus on:

- contribution clarity and unity;
- architecture-level substance;
- evidence-boundary discipline;
- terminology coherence after the v0.5 terminology patches;
- limits of the evaluation framing;
- consistency between prototype-stage claims and non-operational boundaries;
- citation adequacy and citation/proposition fit; and
- residual ambiguous readiness or authority language.

They should also tell reviewers not to evaluate the manuscript as:

- a deployed system;
- a validated governance model;
- a financial, legal, or regulatory instrument; or
- a publication-ready external paper.
