## General Prompt Rules

Each prompt below assumes:

- the reviewer receives the current version-locked package;
- the review remains confidential and internal-only;
- the reviewer must not fill findings outside the assigned role scope unless a
  cross-role issue is material;
- the reviewer must use the assigned role-specific form; and
- no prompt authorizes controlled external review, publication, submission,
  or external circulation.

The package owner should customize only:

- reviewer identifier;
- due date;
- private delivery channel note; and
- any narrowly approved additional file list.

## Prompt A — Architecture/Governance Reviewer

Use with:

- `v0.5-internal-review-form-architecture-governance.md`

Prompt template:

> You are receiving the confidential internal-only `v0.5` review package for
> the Axodus architecture paper in the role of Architecture/Governance
> Reviewer.
>
> Review target:
> - `paper.md`
> - `paper.tex`
> - `references.bib`
>
> Required control documents:
> - `v0.5-internal-review-package.md`
> - `v0.5-internal-review-instructions.md`
> - `publication-readiness.md`
> - `governance/evidence-boundaries.md`
> - `research/terminology.md`
> - `research/bibliography-verified.md`
>
> Your primary task is to evaluate:
> - contribution clarity;
> - constitutional governance framing;
> - bounded local governance domains;
> - functional service modules;
> - contestability framing;
> - architecture-level coherence;
> - whether any passage implies active governance bodies, operational
>   authority, implementation inventory, or deployed institutional
>   capability.
>
> Do not evaluate the manuscript as:
> - a deployed system;
> - an operating institution;
> - a legal or regulatory instrument;
> - a token or financial design;
> - a publication-ready external paper.
>
> Before reviewing:
> 1. Confirm the version-lock hashes in the package manifest.
> 2. Read `governance/evidence-boundaries.md`.
> 3. Use only `v0.5-internal-review-form-architecture-governance.md` to
>    record findings.
>
> Your review should focus on the smallest bounded corrections needed for the
> next internal readiness assessment. Do not edit the manuscript directly.
