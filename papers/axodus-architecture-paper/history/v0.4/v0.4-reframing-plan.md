## Objective

Prepare a controlled conceptual reframing plan for a future v0.4 manuscript
without editing the manuscript itself.

## Working thesis

Axodus is proposed as a prototype-stage conceptual architecture for
traceability and accountability in federated modular digital institutions,
where constitutionally constrained Tenant entities may configure and govern
service nuclei through human-AI-assisted decision processes.

## Core framing shift

The reframing must move the paper from a mostly generic account of human-AI-
assisted digital institutions to a narrower account of:

- constitutional central governance;
- bounded local Tenant autonomy;
- modular service-nucleus selection and governance;
- traceable evidence and provenance requirements;
- review, contestability, and appeal across central and local decisions; and
- human-AI role separation within a federated institutional structure.

## Proposed manuscript outline

1. Title
   A prototype-stage conceptual architecture for traceability and
   accountability in federated modular digital institutions.
2. Abstract
   State the gap around accountable governance across constitutional and
   Tenant-level decisions in modular digital institutions.
3. Introduction
   Define the problem, research gap, contribution, prototype-stage status,
   and out-of-scope boundaries.
4. Background and related work
   Cover provenance, algorithmic accountability, contestability, socio-
   technical coupling, and governance design.
5. Problem statement
   Explain why accountability becomes difficult across central rules, local
   governance, modular services, and AI-assisted analysis.
6. Conceptual architecture
   Define constitutional governance, Tenant domains, service nuclei,
   evidence and provenance, bounded AI assistance, and review paths.
7. Information and control flow
   Explain how constraints, decisions, evidence, recommendations, review, and
   appeals relate conceptually without implying implementation topology.
8. Participation readiness as a supporting mechanism
   Keep participation readiness secondary to the central contribution.
9. Internal maturity distinction
   Include L/D only if needed, and only as an internal operational taxonomy.
10. Evaluation plan
    Keep RQ1 primary and RQ3 secondary, using synthetic, non-deployment
    scenarios.
11. Limitations and future work
    Preserve non-operational, non-validated, and complexity limitations.
12. Conclusion
    Reassert the bounded architectural contribution without public-readiness
    implications.

## Content mapping from v0.3.2

### Retain with limited reframing

- traceability and accountability as the central evaluative axis;
- provenance concepts anchored in W3C PROV-DM;
- contestability, appeal, and accountability literature;
- controlled synthetic examples;
- systems and coupling limitations;
- evidence-boundary discipline; and
- prototype-stage limitations.

### Reframe substantially

- title, abstract, and introduction;
- problem statement and contribution wording;
- architecture sections so federation is primary rather than incidental;
- evaluation scenarios so they test constitutional and Tenant-level
  reconstruction; and
- limitations so they address federation complexity and delegated authority.

### Reduce

- maturity-taxonomy exposition;
- defensive repetition;
- domain-specific DeFi framing; and
- metric lists that outgrow the paper's conceptual scope.

### Exclude from manuscript body

- explicit module inventory;
- product portfolio descriptions;
- named agents;
- tokenomics;
- financial or regulated execution details; and
- any operational or adoption claim.

## Structural editing rules for a future v0.4 phase

- Keep `paper.md` and `paper.tex` structurally synchronized.
- Keep product nuclei abstracted as functional classes or service nuclei.
- Treat the Institutional module inventory as contextual input, not manuscript
  content.
- Preserve all current evidence, publication, and disclosure blockers.
- Do not add new literature to `references.bib` unless verified first.

## Acceptance criteria

A later v0.4 manuscript-editing phase should open only if:

1. The author approves the v0.4 thesis and outline.
2. The author confirms the paper should foreground federated modular
   institutions rather than generic digital institutions.
3. The manuscript continues to abstract away from module inventory,
   tokenomics, and named agents.
4. The retained v0.3.2 literature anchors remain valid for the reframed
   contribution.
5. The readiness report explicitly preserves all external-use blockers.

## Recommendation

Recommend **v0.4 conceptual reframing** as the next manuscript track once the
author explicitly approves this gate. Do not reopen peer review on v0.3.2 and
do not begin v0.4 editing before that approval.
