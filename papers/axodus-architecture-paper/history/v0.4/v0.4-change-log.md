# v0.4 Change Log

Status: Phase 1.18 complete  
Date: 2026-07-04  
Current manuscript version: draft v0.4

## Scope of this revision

Phase 1.18 reframed the manuscript from a generic architecture for
human-AI-assisted digital institutions to a prototype-stage conceptual
architecture for traceability and accountability in federated modular digital
institutions.

## Manuscript changes

- retitled the paper around federated modular digital institutions;
- rewrote the abstract to foreground constitutional governance, bounded
  Tenant autonomy, service nuclei, evidence and provenance, advisory AI
  assistance, and contestability;
- rewrote the introduction so the motivating problem is accountability across
  constitutionally constrained federated modular ecosystems rather than
  generic institutional accountability alone;
- replaced the prior problem statement with a Tenant-and-service-nucleus
  framing centered on traceability, accountability, contestability, and
  evidence boundaries;
- reorganized the architecture section around six layers:
  constitutional governance, Tenant governance, service nuclei, evidence and
  provenance, AI assistance, and risk-boundary or contestability controls;
- rewrote the synthetic scenario as a Tenant module exception review rather
  than a generic policy exception case;
- kept participation readiness as a supporting mechanism and removed any
  manuscript-facing return to internal educational terminology;
- reduced L/D maturity exposure to one bounded internal-operational
  distinction section;
- adapted RQ1 and RQ3 to Tenant-level governance reconstruction and advisory
  human-AI review; and
- strengthened limitations around non-deployment, non-validation, complexity,
  governance overhead, and non-operational boundaries.

## Retained from v0.3.2

- W3C PROV-DM anchoring;
- Leveson-style coupling and systems framing;
- accountable-algorithm and contestable-AI references;
- NIST AI RMF, human-AI interaction, DAO, sociotechnical, and credentialing
  anchors;
- synthetic, non-empirical evaluation framing;
- RQ1 and RQ3 as the primary evaluation path; and
- all publication and external-use blockers.

## Reduced or removed

- generic digital-institution framing where it obscured federation;
- heavy manuscript exposure of the L0-L5 and D0-D5 taxonomy;
- defensive micro-disclaimers repeated inside architecture sections;
- broad supporting-component emphasis;
- DeFi as a central framing device; and
- metric sprawl outside the core traceability and accountability focus.

## Explicit exclusions preserved

- tokenomics, token-distribution mechanics, and token-supply language;
- product cataloging in the manuscript body;
- named agent labels;
- operational, deployment, adoption, user, revenue, treasury, or financial
  claims;
- security guarantees;
- partner, pilot, or institutional-validation claims; and
- public-readiness implications.
