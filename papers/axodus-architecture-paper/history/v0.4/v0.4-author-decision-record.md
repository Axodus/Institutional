## Purpose

This record captures the author decisions that govern the v0.4 manuscript line
after author review of the v0.4 draft. It does not authorize peer review,
controlled external review, publication, submission, outreach, grants,
fundraising use, or authority signaling.

## Confirmed v0.4 thesis

Axodus is proposed as a prototype-stage conceptual architecture for
traceability and accountability in federated modular digital institutions,
where constitutionally constrained Tenant entities may configure and govern
service nuclei through human-AI-assisted decision processes.

## Author decisions

1. **Tenant governance is confirmed.** Tenant governance is an author-approved
   Axodus architectural concept.
2. **Tenant is not the preferred paper-facing term.** In manuscript-facing
   text, the preferred expression is `bounded local governance domain`.
3. **Approved conceptual note for the paper.** The manuscript may use one
   note of the following form or equivalent:

   > Axodus institutional documentation refers to such bounded local
   > governance domains as Tenants. In this paper, the term is used
   > conceptually and does not imply deployed multi-tenant SaaS
   > infrastructure, customer entities, or operating communities.

4. **Service nuclei must not remain a manuscript layer term.** The paper
   should use `functional service modules` as the primary paper-facing term.
5. **Nuclei remain institutional terminology.** `Nuclei` remains acceptable
   for Axodus institutional or product documentation, not for the manuscript's
   horizontal layer model.
6. **Proof of Knowledge stays out of the paper body.** This is a deliberate
   author-approved manuscript policy, not a temporary omission.
7. **The v0.4 title remains unstable.** The title should be treated as
   unsettled until v0.4.1 resolves terminology and architecture layering.
8. **v0.4 remains author-review draft only.** It must not be treated as an
   internal peer-review package until a targeted v0.4.1 patch and a fresh
   readiness package are completed.

## Scope approved for v0.4.1

The manuscript line should continue to focus on:

- federated modular architecture as the institutional object;
- constitutional governance as the central authority layer;
- bounded local governance domains under central constraints;
- functional service modules described as abstract bounded capabilities rather
  than as a product catalog;
- human-AI-assisted governance roles in abstract form only;
- evidence, provenance, contestability, review, appeal, and risk boundaries;
- participation readiness as a supporting mechanism; and
- L-Level and D-Level only as a bounded internal operational distinction if
  required for clarity.
