author review as an **internal working paper**. It is not ready for public

contribution, without enough literature in critical areas, and without
empirical support that would justify broader external claims.

until the author resolves the title, abstract, introduction, `Proof of

## Proposed v0.2 abstract

Axodus is presented here as a prototype-stage conceptual architecture for
studying how digital institutions might connect governance, evidence,
bounded AI assistance, educational participation, and risk controls without
conflating documentation with implementation or automation with legitimate
authority. Existing literatures address socio-technical design,
decentralized governance, human-AI interaction, and participation mechanisms
largely in isolation; what remains under-specified is how these concerns can
be combined in one inspectable institutional model without overstating
operational maturity or validated performance. This paper therefore makes
three bounded contributions: it outlines a layered institutional
architecture, proposes a separation between documentation maturity and
development maturity, and defines a research agenda for evaluating
traceability, participation readiness, human-AI reliance, and governance
risk. Axodus is described only as a conceptual, prototype-stage model. It
does not report a deployed system, users, empirical outcomes, security
validation, or financial performance. `Proof of Knowledge` is treated here
only as a proposed educational participation and governance-readiness
mechanism, not as a cryptographic protocol or financial mechanism.

## Revised introduction outline for v0.2

1. Opening problem
   State that digital institutions often combine governance, documentation,
   automation, and participation mechanisms without a clear integrative model
   for authority, evidence, and accountability.
2. Research gap
   State in 2-4 sentences that current literatures treat socio-technical
   design, DAO governance, human-AI oversight, and participation mechanisms
   separately, leaving a gap in inspectable integrative institutional models.
3. Paper scope
   State that Axodus is introduced only as a prototype-stage conceptual
   architecture and research agenda, not as a validated implementation.
4. Contribution statement
   State the three bounded contributions in compact form.
5. Scope exclusions
   State explicitly that the paper does not validate educational mechanisms,
   AI reliability, governance effectiveness, operational readiness, or any
   financial/token mechanism.
6. Paper map
   Briefly preview architecture, participation/governance-readiness framing,
   maturity distinction, evaluation priorities, and limitations.

## Recommendation on `Proof of Knowledge`

Recommended disposition: **used only as internal Axodus terminology**.

Editorial reasoning:

- The term remains too easily confused with a cryptographic proof-of-knowledge
  protocol.
- The term also carries latent non-academic associations because its fuller
  Axodus design context extends beyond what this paper should describe.
- For v0.2 manuscript text, use a generic phrase such as `educational
  participation and governance-readiness mechanism`, and if the internal term
  must appear at all, introduce it once as an Axodus-internal label with a
  strong disclaimer.

## Recommendation on `Academy`

Recommended disposition: **replaced by `educational layer`**.

Editorial reasoning:

- `Academy` reads like a branded institutional subsystem rather than a generic
  conceptual layer.
- `Educational layer` is narrower, less presumptive, and easier to defend in a
  conceptual architecture paper.
- If the internal label must be preserved for repository continuity, keep it in
  internal notes, not as a public-facing manuscript term.

## Sections to narrow, merge, or defer

- **Abstract**: narrow to the core gap, prototype-stage scope, and bounded
  contribution.
- **Introduction**: rewrite around one explicit research gap instead of several
  parallel motivations.
- **Background and Related Work**: narrow to the literatures required to
  defend the paper's integrative claim; defer broader adjacent motivations.
- **Proof of Knowledge and Educational Participation**: retain only the
  educational/governance-readiness framing; remove manuscript dependence on
  branded internal terminology where possible.
- **Risk Boundaries and Treasury Transparency**: rename and narrow to `Risk
  Boundaries`; defer treasury-specific framing.
- **Evaluation Plan** and **Research Agenda**: tighten or partially merge so
  they present a more prioritized sequence rather than a large open program.

## Content to defer from the architecture paper

The following should be deferred to a separate PoK/tokenomics technical note
after legal, economic, and empirical review:

- any linkage between educational progression and token issuance or release;
- minting logic, release logic, or supply-allocation logic;
- scarcity, value-accrual, or circulating-supply design claims;
- incentive and behavioral claims tied to token-linked participation;
- governance rights or eligibility effects tied to token mechanics;
- legal, regulatory, economic, or market analysis of token-linked educational
  gating.
