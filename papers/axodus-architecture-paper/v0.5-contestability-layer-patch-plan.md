## Current Role of Section 4.6

Section `4.6` currently functions as the conceptual layer that frames:

- risk boundaries;
- contestability;
- challenge conditions;
- review visibility;
- escalation as a conceptual design affordance; and
- limits of nominal human oversight.

Its current role is to describe where challenge, correction, review, and
escalation remain conceptually visible after a proposed or recorded decision.
It must continue to be treated as a design layer rather than as an operating
appeals, moderation, enforcement, or compliance procedure.

## Planned Modification Type

- controlled contestability-boundary refinement

## Allowed Intervention

Future Section `4.6` work may only:

- clarify contestability as a conceptual review and challenge surface;
- clarify that risk boundaries identify where further review, escalation,
  refusal, or documentation may be needed;
- distinguish contestability from an operating appeals process;
- distinguish reviewability from binding review authority;
- distinguish escalation as a conceptual design affordance from an active
  escalation workflow;
- clarify that contestability supports accountability by making decisions and
  interventions reconstructable and challengeable in principle;
- clarify that human oversight must be meaningful and reviewable, but not
  claim that the system already implements such oversight; and
- align Section `4.6` with the already-refined framing in Sections `4.1`,
  `4.4`, and `4.5`.

## High-Risk Terms and Safer Alternatives

| High-risk term | Safer alternative or rule |
| --- | --- |
| `appeal` | Prefer `challenge path` or `review condition` unless explicitly framed as non-operational. |
| `escalation` | Prefer `escalation condition` or `review trigger` and avoid active workflow implication. |
| `enforcement` | Avoid; use `constraint` or `boundary condition`. |
| `review board` | Avoid; use `review role` or `review surface` only if conceptual. |
| `moderation` | Avoid; use `risk review` only if conceptual. |
| `decision authority` | Avoid unless negated; use `decision context` or `accountability boundary`. |
| `independent reviewer` | Use carefully; clarify that independence is a design concern, not a named active role. |
| `process` | Use carefully; prefer `design surface`, `condition`, or `mechanism` when non-operational. |

## Claim-Boundary Risks

The future Section `4.6` patch must treat the following as live risks:

- contestability can sound like a functioning appeals system;
- escalation can sound like an operational workflow;
- review can sound like binding institutional authority;
- human oversight can become nominal if not connected to traceability and
  reconstructability;
- risk boundaries can sound like implemented controls or safety guarantees; and
- independence language can sound like a staffed institutional role.

## Proposed Future Patch Shape

The future Section `4.6` patch should:

- keep the section compact;
- avoid adding new subsections unless necessary;
- avoid new citations;
- avoid new examples that imply real users, real cases, deployed systems, or
  operational procedures;
