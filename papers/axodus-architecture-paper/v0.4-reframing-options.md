# v0.4 Reframing Options

Status: Phase 1.16A planning record  
v0.4 manuscript opened: no  
Manuscript edits authorized: no

## Decision context

The author clarification changes the institutional object described by the
paper. Axodus is intended as a federated modular ecosystem with central
constitutional constraints, bounded Tenant autonomy, optional service nuclei,
and separated AI-assistance roles. The current v0.3.2 manuscript contains
reusable accountability mechanisms but does not make that federation the
organizing premise.

## Options

| Option | Assessment | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| v0.3.3 targeted technical patch | Would require premise-level changes across most major sections and would misclassify a substantial reframing as a minor patch | Not recommended |
| v0.4 conceptual reframing | Can preserve the validated v0.3.2 material while rebuilding the problem, architecture, and evaluation around federated modular institutions | Recommended, not yet authorized |
| Separate whitepaper first | Would permit broader product and economic description but would not resolve the academic paper's conceptual mismatch | Deferred; not a prerequisite |
| No manuscript edits | Preserves the historical draft but leaves it misaligned with the author-defined ecosystem | Appropriate only until the author approves or rejects v0.4 |

## Recommended v0.4 direction

Working contribution:

> A prototype-stage conceptual architecture for traceability and
> accountability in federated modular digital institutions, combining central
> constitutional constraints, bounded Tenant governance, optional service
> nuclei, provenance requirements, and human-authorized AI assistance.

The recommendation preserves traceability and accountability as the academic
contribution while defining the decisions they govern: delegation from the
constitutional layer, Tenant-level configuration, service-nucleus selection,
AI-assisted analysis, human disposition, and review or appeal.

## Proposed boundaries

A v0.4 plan should:

- use `Tenant` as a defined federated governance domain rather than a generic
  SaaS tenancy;
- model service nuclei through abstract contracts, not a product catalog;
- use abstract AI-assistance roles rather than Morpheus, Agent Smith, or
  Trinity;
- keep the complete inventory in controlled Institutional documentation;
- exclude tokenomics, token distribution, financial strategies, revenue,
  treasury, and regulated execution;
- retain prototype-stage and non-operational limitations;
- preserve human decision authority and separate production and sensitive-
  action authorization from maturity; and
- propose synthetic evaluation before any institutional validation claim.

## Author decisions required before v0.4

The author must separately approve:

1. opening v0.4 as a new manuscript revision;
2. the exact title and contribution statement;
3. the definition and public use of `Tenant`;
4. the future layer and authority model;
5. the boundary between the academic paper and institutional portfolio
   documentation; and
6. any new literature-verification work needed for federalism, multilevel
   governance, platform modularity, delegation, and institutional design.

## Recommendation

Recommend **v0.4 conceptual reframing**. Do not open v0.4, modify the
manuscript, or restart peer review until a separate author decision authorizes
that work.
