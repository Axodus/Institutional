## Purpose

This package records the version-locked internal manuscript state used for the
confidential `v0.5` review cycle and preserved for the closed `v0.5`
internal baseline.

This package is not a publication package. It does not authorize controlled
external review, publication, submission, outreach, grant use, fundraising
use, external distribution, or authority signaling.

## Package scope

The package is designed to preserve the reviewed baseline for internal use in:

- contribution clarity;
- architecture-level substance;
- evidence-boundary discipline;
- sensitive-claim control;
- Markdown/LaTeX consistency;
- citation and bibliography discipline; and
- readiness posture for later internal baseline or readiness decisions.

The package is not designed for review of:

- implementation evidence;
- production operation;
- tokenomics;
- financial design;
- legal or regulatory compliance;
- external publication readiness; or
- private technical or operational materials.

## Review focus questions

Reviewers should answer the following with section references and severity
ratings where a problem is identified.

### Contribution and focus

1. Is the central contribution stated clearly and consistently?
2. Does the paper remain centered on traceability, accountability,
   contestability, and bounded human--AI-assisted institutional design?
3. Do participation readiness, maturity distinctions, and governance
   boundaries remain supporting elements rather than competing contributions?
4. Is the paper's prototype-stage conceptual classification credible?

### Architecture and terminology

5. Are constitutional governance, bounded local governance domains,
   functional service modules, evidence and provenance, advisory AI
   assistance, and contestability differentiated clearly?
6. Do any passages imply implementation inventory, active governance bodies,
   operational authority, or deployed institutional capability?
7. Is the terminology coherent after the v0.5 terminology cleanup?
8. Does any remaining wording still sound too operational, validated, or
   externally mature?

### Evidence, claims, and consistency

9. Does any statement exceed `governance/evidence-boundaries.md`?
10. Are facts, design proposals, internal taxonomy, limitations, and future
    work distinguishable?
11. Are remaining negative boundary statements clear and non-contradictory?
12. Are `paper.md` and `paper.tex` substantively synchronized?

### Citations and readiness

13. Does each cited source support the proposition for which it is used?
14. Are there any citation-use mismatches between Markdown and LaTeX?
15. Are any readiness labels, blockers, or package-control statements
    inconsistent?
16. What is the smallest revision set required before the next internal
    readiness assessment?

## Recommended reviewer profile

A complete internal review should include two to four reviewers with
complementary expertise:

- a socio-technical systems or institutional-governance reviewer;
- a human--AI interaction, calibrated-reliance, or evaluation-methods
  reviewer;
- a systems-architecture, traceability, provenance, or assurance reviewer;
- optionally, a contestability, governance-process, or disclosure-boundary
  reviewer.

Reviewers should be able to distinguish conceptual architecture from
implementation evidence and should not have direct authorship responsibility
for the passages they primarily assess. Conflicts of interest, prior project
involvement, and limits of expertise must be disclosed in the review form.

## Package control

The package owner should:

- assign each copy to a named internal reviewer;
- record the exact files and hashes supplied;
- use an approved private transfer and return channel;
- set a return or deletion date;
- avoid public links, public issue trackers, and unrestricted shared drives;
- collect completed forms without exposing reviewer identities to other
  reviewers unless explicitly approved; and
- record findings before considering any manuscript or readiness change.

No review result, including a favorable result, changes the current external
publication blockers automatically.
