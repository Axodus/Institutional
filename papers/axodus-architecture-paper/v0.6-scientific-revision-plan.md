## Revision Principles

Any later scientific revision must preserve prototype-stage and conceptual
status, distinguish design proposals from supported facts, avoid operational
maturity or validation claims, keep bibliography expansion closed unless
separately reopened, preserve Markdown/LaTeX synchronization, and require a
post-patch audit before any readiness advancement.

It must not introduce operational governance mechanics, Academy operational or
financial mechanics, tokenomics, treasury, marketplace, minting, payout,
fundraising, investor, product, infrastructure, production, private-governance,
implementation, adoption, validation, or superiority claims.

## SR-01 - Conceptual Synthesis Method and Derivation

Future revision direction:

- describe the method as a narrative, non-systematic conceptual synthesis
  unless the author chooses a different substantiated method;
- map literature strands and the five tensions to explicit design
  requirements;
- map those requirements to the six parts or layers;
- state whether the decomposition is provisional, useful, minimal,
  sufficient, or necessary;
- identify why layer boundaries are drawn where they are;
- distinguish module capabilities from cross-layer interfaces and controls;
  and
- avoid claiming empirical validation or implementation.

## SR-02 - Novelty and Gap Strategy

Allowed path A: bounded synthesis.

- Replace broad absence language with a narrower claim that the paper develops
  one proposed integration of differently scoped components.
- Avoid asserting that no relevant architecture exists unless a bounded
  comparison supports that claim.
- No new source is required solely for this path.

Allowed path B: substantiated comparison.

- Retain a stronger gap claim only with an explicit review scope and
  comparison to closest alternatives.
- Any new source requires separate candidate evaluation, metadata
  verification, proposition-fit assessment, and patch authorization.

Default safest path for revision planning: path A, unless the author explicitly
chooses path B.

## SR-03 - Evaluation and Construct Alignment

Future revision must either narrow the contribution so RQ1 and RQ3 are
explicitly tests of reconstructability, traceability, evidence-model use, and
bounded reliance, or revise the research questions so the integrated
architecture, accountability-supporting conditions, and contestability
constructs are testable at the conceptual or artifact-evaluation level.

Required safeguards:

- do not claim accountability effectiveness is demonstrated;
- distinguish accountability-supporting conditions from institutional
  answerability, remedy, reversal, or consequence;
- resolve RQ numbering by either renumbering RQ3 or explicitly identifying a
  deferred RQ2; and
- keep all evaluation language future-oriented unless a study is actually
  performed and separately evidenced.

## SR-04 - Participation Readiness and Internal Maturity Scope

Future revision options:

- compress Sections `5` and `6` into short support/boundary subsections;
- retain them as sections only with explicit rationale for how they support
  the central contribution;
- move most content to future work or limitations;
- connect them to a future research question; or
- remove project-specific maturity taxonomy from the main argumentative path.

Boundaries:

- Brennan and epistocracy remain excluded unless separately reopened;
- Section `5` must not drift into Academy operations, access-control
  mechanics, treasury, minting, payout, marketplace, tokenomics, or
  credentialist authority claims;
- conceptual limitations must not become product requirements.

## SR-05 - Architecture or Framework Framing

If the author retains `conceptual architecture`, the future revision should
add at least a minimal structural artifact such as a layer
responsibility/interface table, input/output/dependency table, invariant and
failure-condition table, or derivation matrix linking requirements to layers.

If the author reframes to `conceptual framework`, the future revision must
propagate the wording consistently through title, abstract, introduction,
Section `4`, and conclusion without weakening existing evidence boundaries.

The revision must not imply implementation architecture, software topology,
deployment architecture, operating governance, or validated system design.

## SR-06 - Multi-Year Effort Wording

The phrase permitted by evidence boundaries is:

> The project originates from a multi-year independent research and design
> effort.

Future revision may retain that sentence if it remains clearly classified as
author-provided background and not evidence of validation. If used to explain
derivation, the manuscript must clarify what role the effort played in
conceptual synthesis without inventing artifacts, data, metrics, users,
adoption, implementation, or field evidence.

## SR-07 - Internal Review Disclosure Boundary

Future external-facing package records must not call internal tool-assisted
reviews independent human peer review.

Required package-language direction:

- describe these materials as internal, tool-assisted scientific review
  inputs when disclosed at all;
- do not imply external peer review, journal peer review, human independence,
  endorsement, validation, acceptance, certification, or publication approval;
  and
- keep the materials excluded from any reviewer package unless a later
  disclosure decision authorizes them.
