## Gate basis

The v0.3.1 patch:

- makes the conceptual component architecture more explicit without claiming
  implementation;
- distinguishes organizational/governance maturity from
  development/implementation maturity without presenting either as a
  validated scientific measure;
- removes internal PoK terminology from the academic manuscript;
- connects RQ1 and RQ3 to a controlled simulated evaluation environment;
- narrows the Schär citation to its DeFi domain;
- reconciles the manuscript's reference-verification statement with the
  remaining metadata-recheck requirement;
- records the author-approved contact exposure and recognized contact risks;
- preserves prototype-stage, evidence, IP, and publication boundaries.

## Internal peer-review scope

Internal reviewers should assess:

- whether the functional classes form a coherent traceability path;
- whether authority, evidence, AI provenance, rationale, conflict disclosure,
  and appeal are sufficiently differentiated;
- whether the L/D distinction clarifies rather than distracts from the central
  contribution;
- whether the simulated environment can test RQ1 and RQ3 without implying a
  deployed platform;
- whether repeated defensive language has been reduced without creating an
  overclaim;
- whether remaining bibliography gaps prevent any later external review.

  contestability and appeals, calibrated reliance, assessment validity, DAO
  participation, maturity-model reliability, and Web3 regulatory constraints.
- The conceptual architecture has no empirical validation or operational
  evidence.
- The maturity taxonomy has not been empirically validated for reliability.
- The proposed architecture may be too complex to govern in practice without
  substantial scope reduction.
- Author, licensing, affiliation, disclosure, venue, and third-party-rights
  decisions remain unresolved for external use.
- Independent internal review findings have not yet been recorded or resolved.
