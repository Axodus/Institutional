without broadening its central contribution or changing its prototype-stage

## Targeted manuscript scope

### 1. Contribution statement

Clarify that the paper's synthesis is the proposed connection among decision
authority, evidence, attributable AI assistance, rationale, contestability,
and review. Do not make novelty or comparative-superiority claims.

### 2. Minimum architecture flow

Add a concise conceptual sequence:

1. a role receives bounded authority;
2. a decision request identifies scope and applicable rules;
3. evidence and conflicts are recorded;
4. AI assistance, when used, is attributable to its task and source material;
5. a responsible human records disposition, decision, and rationale; and
6. an independent review or appeal path can inspect and challenge the record.

Describe dependency direction, information flow, and control handoffs without
specifying software topology or claiming implementation.

### 3. Controlled illustrations

Add no more than two short synthetic examples. Each example must be labeled
illustrative and non-empirical. It must not name an actual Axodus module,
institution, partner, user, deployment, or operational event.

Prefer non-financial examples such as review of a policy exception or review
of an AI-assisted document classification.

### 4. Provenance and accountability

Define candidate provenance fields and representations at the conceptual
level. Potential fields include source reference, transformation or model
role, timestamp category, responsible reviewer, disposition, and linkage to
decision rationale.

Do not imply implemented logs, hashes, cryptographic guarantees, immutable
records, security properties, or production controls. Add scholarly or
standards citations only after verification.

### 5. Contestability

Specify design requirements for:

- reviewer independence and recusal;
- separation between original decision and appeal;
- review capacity and escalation when demand exceeds capacity;
- correction of evidence and rationale records;
- capture resistance through role rotation or multi-role review as hypotheses;
  and
- preservation of an appeal path proportionate to decision consequence.

Do not claim these requirements are effective or implemented.

### 6. Complexity reduction

Add scope-reduction principles:

- minimum sufficient decision record;
- proportional controls based on consequence;
- optional supporting layers rather than mandatory universal machinery;
- explicit dependency budgets or interface limits as design hypotheses; and
- removal of a component when it does not improve reconstructability or
  contestability in evaluation.

### 7. Participation readiness

Keep participation readiness supporting and concise. Add only candidate
evaluation criteria and safeguards, such as false exclusion, accessibility,
appeal availability, curriculum concentration, privacy burden, and evidence
that the mechanism improves the targeted decision context.

Do not claim mitigation effectiveness or educational validity.

### 8. Evaluation path

Retain synthetic evaluation as the initial stage. Describe later stages only
as future work:

1. controlled artifact inspection;
2. bounded reviewer studies using synthetic scenarios;
3. controlled prototype evaluation under an approved protocol; and
4. possible institutional validation only after separate governance, ethics,
   legal, privacy, and evidence gates.

Define proposed measures rather than operational metrics. Candidate measures
may include reconstruction accuracy, missing-evidence detection, review time,
reviewer disagreement, planted-error detection, override behavior, appeal
completion, and failure to identify authority conflicts.

### 9. Scope and terminology cleanup

- Remove or subordinate the Schär/DeFi dependency example.
- Reduce repeated defensive disclaimers in architecture sections.
- Resolve the abstract/Section 5 maturity imbalance without importing the full
  taxonomy.
- Preserve the complete maturity taxonomy as internal governance material.
- Keep financial, token, internal-brand, implementation, and authority claims
  out of the manuscript.
