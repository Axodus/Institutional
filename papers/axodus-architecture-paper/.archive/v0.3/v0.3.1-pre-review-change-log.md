# v0.3.1 Pre-Review Change Log

Date: 2026-07-03  
Scope: Internal pre-review patch only  
Result: Ready for internal peer review

## Purpose

The v0.3.1 patch strengthens architectural and methodological substance while
preserving the paper's focus on traceability and accountability in
human–AI-assisted digital institutions. It does not open a v0.4 or authorize
external circulation.

## Manuscript changes

### Metadata and references

- Updated the draft label from v0.3 to v0.3.1.
- Changed the status from author-review gating to internal-peer-review
  readiness while retaining explicit external-circulation and publication
  blocks.
- Clarified in both manuscript forms that cited references have internal
  verification records but still require final metadata rechecking before any
  submission, public release, or external circulation.

### Architectural substance

- Expanded Section 4.3 with a minimal conceptual taxonomy of functional
  component types: authority and role registry, evidence registry,
  AI-assistance provenance layer, decision-rationale record, conflict and
  disclosure register, review and appeal path, risk-boundary controller, and
  participation-readiness support.
- Identified those items as conceptual functional classes rather than a
  definitive module inventory or implementation claim.
- Connected the classes as an inspectable path from authority and evidence
  through assistance and rationale to review or appeal.
- Replaced defensive wording in Section 4.6 with specific boundary fields:
  responsible role, triggering condition, required evidence, permitted
  exception, and escalation path.

### Maturity taxonomy

- Replaced the earlier documentation/development shorthand with two
  orthogonal internal operational dimensions.
- Defined L-Level as organizational, documentation, governance, and authority
  maturity.
- Defined D-Level as development, implementation, technical validation, and
  operational maturity.
- Stated that L-Level does not measure code, D-Level does not measure
  governance, and neither dimension authorizes production or sensitive action.
- Identified the rubric as internal and not empirically validated.
- Kept individual level definitions out of the paper and placed the complete
  rubric in `../../governance/maturity-taxonomy.md`.

### Terminology and scope

- Removed the internal `Proof of Knowledge` terminology note from the
  manuscript body.
- Retitled Section 6 as `Participation Readiness Mechanism`.
- Used descriptive terms including `educational readiness` and `bounded
  participation`.
- Removed repeated out-of-scope financial and token-related language from the
  introduction, risk section, and research agenda without adding any such
  content.

### Related work and evaluation

- Reframed the Schär citation as a DeFi-specific example of composability and
  dependency propagation, not general evidence about all digital
  infrastructures.
- Added a required controlled experimental environment before RQ1 or RQ3:
  synthetic decision scenarios, controlled evidence packages, simulated
  AI-assistance records, planted errors, and a high-fidelity mock-up or
  wizard-of-oz interface.
- Added protocol criteria for reconstruction accuracy, completion time,
  reviewer disagreement, missing-evidence detection, error detection, and
  override behavior.
- Replaced the defensive conclusion with a concrete next research step.
