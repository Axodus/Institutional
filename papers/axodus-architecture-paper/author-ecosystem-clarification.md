# Author Ecosystem Clarification

Status: Phase 1.16A controlled institutional disclosure  
Source: author clarification supplied by Mauricio  
Evidence status: author-provided design context, not implementation evidence

## Purpose

This record corrects the editorial understanding of Axodus. The project is not
intended merely as a generic architecture for human--AI-assisted digital
institutions. Its author-defined design is a federated modular ecosystem in
which a central constitutional layer constrains Tenant-level governance and
the selection of independent but integrated product or service nuclei.

This document may name intended nuclei as controlled institutional disclosure
under `../../governance/public-disclosure-boundary.md`. It does not establish
implementation, integration, maturity, production, adoption, revenue,
security, or regulatory approval.

## Corrected ecosystem model

The author clarification establishes the following design intent:

- Axodus is conceived as an ecosystem of independent and integrated nuclei
  governed by a central constitutional layer.
- A Tenant is a federated governance domain with a bounded community,
  delegated decision authority, and the ability to select service nuclei while
  remaining subject to central constitutional constraints.
- Tenant communities may govern local configurations and priorities. Economic
  parameters such as rates, contributions, or weights require separate
  evidence and legal review and are not part of the current academic paper.
- A nucleus is conceived as a modular product or service that a Tenant may
  include in its configuration, subject to explicit authority, evidence,
  review, and risk boundaries.
- Traceability and accountability apply across constitutional delegation,
  Tenant decisions, module selection, AI assistance, evidence records, and
  review or appeal.

## Author-defined AI roles

The author uses three working labels for the intended separation of AI-agent
responsibilities:

| Working label | Author-defined role | Safe abstract role |
|---|---|---|
| Morpheus | Constitutional knowledge and ecosystem-guardian role | Constitutional analysis and institutional-knowledge support |
| Agent Smith | Adversarial, security, and compliance-sentinel role | Adversarial assurance and policy-conformance review |
| Trinity | Operational, financial, planning, and analytical role | Operational planning and analysis under explicit execution gates |

These names are controlled institutional context pending branding, trademark,
IP, and legal review. They should not enter the academic paper. The role
descriptions do not imply deployed agents, autonomous authority, implemented
controls, security guarantees, or permission to execute financial or other
sensitive actions.

## Academy and token boundary

The author describes Academy as strategically relevant to educational
qualification and governance-token distribution. That relationship is
tokenomics-only context. Token allocation, minting, supply, scarcity,
incentives, and financial interpretation remain outside the architecture
paper and require separate legal, economic, empirical, and publication review.

## Editorial consequence

The current v0.3.2 manuscript remains useful as a record of traceability,
accountability, provenance, contestability, and evaluation concepts. It does
not adequately represent the author-defined federated modular premise.
Internal peer review of v0.3.2 is therefore paused pending an author decision
on a v0.4 conceptual reframing.
