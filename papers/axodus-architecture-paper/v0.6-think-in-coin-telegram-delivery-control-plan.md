## Channel controls

A future delivery must use a private, access-controlled group or channel with named or otherwise approved and verified reviewers. Membership in Think in Coin is not by itself evidence of reviewer qualification. The setup must use no public invite link, limit administrative privileges, pin the package instructions, and define a review window. Reviewers may be asked as a professional courtesy not to redistribute or publicly discuss the manuscript before review closure, but that request does not modify or restrict CC BY 4.0 rights.

Telegram is a private coordination channel, not a legally secure or technically confidential document repository. The repository or another controlled record remains the source of truth for package hashes, assignments, declarations, returned reviews, author dispositions, and closure decisions. Screenshots, forwarding, local copies, account compromise, platform retention, and unauthorized disclosure remain residual risks; no absolute confidentiality or non-copying guarantee is claimed.

## Required pre-delivery checks

1. Phase 2.17 identifies each reviewer, eligibility basis, assigned language, package hashes, purpose, scope, review period, and return channel.
2. Conflict and process-confidentiality declarations are accepted and recorded without purporting to restrict CC BY 4.0 manuscript rights.
3. Access is individual and least privilege; no public or anonymous shared link is used.
4. The pinned message identifies the English manuscript as authoritative and the pt-BR copy as a controlled translation.
5. Reviewers receive only the approved package, not repository-wide access, private branches, issues, credentials, infrastructure, or internal deliberations.

## Return and closure

Reviews must be returned through the assigned private channel or controlled form. Original returns are retained immutably and indexed without erasing disagreement. After the review window, access is removed or archived according to the Phase 2.17 record, and reviewers receive a retention or deletion request where applicable.

## Incident procedure

Unauthorized disclosure of process-confidential information, account compromise, or package-hash mismatch requires: suspend further delivery; preserve evidence; identify affected records and recipients; notify the responsible human; revoke private-channel access where possible; record the incident; and issue a supersession or containment decision. Manuscript redistribution contrary to the courtesy request may be recorded as a process-integrity event but must not be characterized as a violation of rights granted by CC BY 4.0.
