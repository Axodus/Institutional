# v0.3 Editorial Plan

Planning date: 2026-07-03  
Scope: editorial direction only for the next internal manuscript revision

## Objective

Narrow the paper's contribution so that v0.3 is more precise than v0.2
without becoming more ambitious. The dominant contribution axis should be
**traceability and accountability in human-AI-assisted digital institutions**.

## Core contribution for v0.3

Primary contribution:

- a prototype-stage conceptual architecture for making authority, evidence,
  bounded AI assistance, decision rationale, and review paths more
  inspectable in digital institutions.

Secondary supporting components:

- participation readiness mechanism;
- governance framing;
- risk boundaries;
- maturity separation;
- broader research agenda.

These supporting components should remain in the manuscript only insofar as
they support the central traceability/accountability argument.

## Contribution hierarchy

1. Central:
   traceability, evidence linkage, decision records, human oversight,
   contestability, and accountability under human-AI-assisted institutional
   decision making.
2. Secondary:
   bounded AI assistance and review conditions.
3. Supporting:
   participation readiness mechanism, governance framing, risk boundaries, and
   maturity separation.
4. Deferred:
   broader institutional-program ambitions not necessary to defend the central
   contribution.

## Section priorities for v0.3

Sections that should become more central:

- Introduction
- Problem Statement
- Conceptual Architecture
- Governance, Accountability, and Human Oversight
- Evaluation Plan
- Limitations and Threats to Validity

Sections that should be reduced, merged, or moved toward future work:

- `Educational Participation and Governance Readiness`
  Reduce and present as a supporting mechanism rather than a co-equal
  contribution.
- `Modularity and Maturity`
  Reduce and keep only the part needed to prevent documentation/implementation
  overclaiming.
- `Risk Boundaries`
  Reduce and tie more directly to accountability and reviewability.
- `Research Agenda`
  Merge more tightly with the evaluation plan or shorten substantially.
- Portions of `Background and Related Work`
  Rebalance toward literature directly relevant to traceability,
  accountability, contestability, and calibrated reliance.

## Evaluation plan reordering

Primary initial study:

- `RQ1 — Traceability`

Secondary study:

- `RQ3 — Human-AI reliance`

Future work unless tightly scoped:

- `RQ2 — Educational participation`
- `RQ4 — Maturity reliability`

- RQ3 should be retained only as a clearly secondary study.
- RQ2, RQ4, and RQ5 should be reframed as later-stage investigations unless
  they can be scoped narrowly enough to avoid diluting the main contribution.
