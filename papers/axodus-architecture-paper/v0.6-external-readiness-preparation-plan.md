## Definition of Controlled External Review

Controlled external review means limited distribution of a fixed,
specifically approved package to specifically approved reviewers for bounded
academic, technical, institutional, or methodological feedback. It operates
under explicit confidentiality, attribution, use, retention, and
redistribution rules.

It does not imply publication, submission, endorsement, partnership,
certification, validation, acceptance, or public release. It is distinct from:

- public circulation or open peer review;
- journal or conference submission;
- partner due diligence;
- investor review or fundraising disclosure;
- legal review or security audit;
- product validation;
- institutional endorsement; and
- publication acceptance.

The phrase `external review` must not be used externally without the approved
`controlled external review` qualifier and protocol.

## Readiness Principles

All later controlled-review preparation must apply:

- least disclosure and purpose limitation;
- fixed-version review and one package source of truth;
- named accountability and explicit reviewer scope;
- informed reviewer consent;
- no implied endorsement;
- no onward redistribution;
- no use for investment, fundraising, production, legal, or regulatory
  reliance;
- explicit distinction among facts, hypotheses, conceptual proposals, and
  future work;
- reversible authorization;
- a complete audit trail; and
- an explicit human decision before every external step.

## External-Readiness Gate Architecture

### Gate ER-1 - Internal Academic Integrity

ER-1 confirms closed bibliography and editorial workstreams, citation
integrity, Markdown/LaTeX parity, absence of unresolved major manuscript
defects, and preservation of claims and evidence boundaries.

### Gate ER-2 - Metadata and Document Identity

ER-2 confirms title, version, date, author, affiliation, contact, document
type, license, status legend, review-copy marking, canonical filename, package
identifier, and document hash.

### Gate ER-3 - Disclosure Boundary

ER-3 confirms public/private separation and excludes confidential operational
data, private identifiers, financial or token-related mechanics, Academy
operations, security-sensitive detail, and unapproved relationship claims.

### Gate ER-4 - Legal and Rights Review

ER-4 records required qualified human or legal decisions concerning
copyright, licensing, quotations, marks, author rights, affiliation,
confidentiality, data protection, reviewer terms, jurisdiction, liability,
and reliance disclaimers. It remains unresolved in this phase.

### Gate ER-5 - Institutional and Reputational Readiness

ER-5 confirms accurate manuscript positioning, no maturity exaggeration, no
implied endorsement, no misleading peer-review claim, policy consistency, and
bounded professional reviewer communication.

### Gate ER-6 - Reviewer Governance

ER-6 confirms eligibility, independence, conflicts, scope, confidentiality,
attribution, deadlines, redistribution restrictions, and retention rules.

### Gate ER-7 - Reviewer Package Readiness

ER-7 confirms a complete, consistent, fixed, hashed, and human-approved
package. Planning artifacts do not satisfy package approval.

### Gate ER-8 - Human Authorization

ER-8 requires explicit human approval of package version, reviewer identity,
disclosure level, protocol, transmission method, timing, purpose, and review
scope. No external action may occur without ER-8.

## Reviewer Eligibility and Independence

Permitted reviewer profiles may include academic, systems/governance,
technical architecture, institutional-design, methodology, or independent
interdisciplinary reviewers. No person is identified in this phase.

Every reviewer must have relevant expertise, ability to assess the assigned
scope, no unresolved conflict, no unapproved compensation expectation, no
authority to represent Axodus, no redistribution right, no partnership or
endorsement right, protocol acceptance, and willingness to separate academic
feedback from legal, financial, product, or investment advice.

## Conflict-of-Interest Controls

Before eligibility can be decided, a reviewer must declare financial or
investment interests, employment or advisory relationships, competitor and
personal relationships, contribution interests, IP disputes, commercial and
institutional conflicts, prior access to private Axodus material, and intent
to publish or reuse content.

Allowed human decisions are `eligible`, `eligible with disclosed limitation`,
`restricted scope`, `recusal`, and `ineligible`. Every eligibility decision
requires explicit human approval and an internal record.

## Feedback, Attribution, and Decision Protocol

A future approved process must:

1. log receipt, reviewer identity, and assigned scope;
2. classify feedback as critical, major, minor, advisory, or out of scope;
3. request evidence or rationale where needed;
4. record the author decision as accept, partially accept, defer, or reject;
5. require a separate approved patch for manuscript changes;
6. obtain explicit consent before reviewer attribution or quotation;
7. prohibit representing any comment as endorsement; and
8. prohibit automatic publication after review completion.

Reviewer anonymity may be offered externally, but identity must remain
internally traceable to the responsible human and protected under the approved
retention and access policy.

## Versioning, Evidence, and Audit Trail

A future package requires a fixed commit or repository state, fixed PDF,
SHA-256, package version, manifest, recipient log, transmission date, scope,
declarations, protocol acceptance, feedback receipt, author decision, and
revocation or supersession record.

No file may be silently replaced and no stale package may be reused. The
package manifest and its hash record are the single source of truth for what a
reviewer was permitted to receive.

## Security and Access-Control Requirements

Future execution must use least privilege, individual recipient delivery, no
public link, no anonymous shared folder, revocable or expiring access where
available, no repository-wide access by default, no access to secrets,
credentials, CI variables, private issues or branches, recipient-specific
records, bounded retention, and an incident process for leakage or
unauthorized redistribution.

No such distribution mechanism is selected or claimed implemented in Phase
`2.11`.
