## Central Thesis

The revised thesis is clear and proportionate: Axodus proposes an integrated
six-layer conceptual architecture for making authority, modular capability,
evidence, advisory AI input, and challenge conditions inspectable in
constitutionally constrained federated digital institutions. Traceability,
reconstructability, and bounded review are positioned as conditions that may
support accountability and contestability, not as proof that those
institutional properties have been achieved.

This is a defensible thesis for a conceptual architecture and focused research-
agenda paper.

## Contribution and Novelty

The contribution is now consistently an integrative synthesis rather than a
literature-wide absence finding. The abstract, introduction, method, and
conclusion identify the same six responsibilities and explicitly state that
the decomposition is one defensible arrangement among alternatives.

This change materially reduces novelty overclaim. The manuscript does not
need to prove that no adjacent architecture exists in order to contribute a
review-oriented synthesis. A human reviewer may still ask for comparison with
specific neighboring frameworks, but that is now a normal review question,
not a contradiction between the claimed method and claimed novelty.

## Conceptual Method

Section `2.6` supplies the previously missing derivation procedure. It states
the method type, its non-systematic scope, the role of the five tensions, the
reason for separating layer responsibilities, and the non-necessity of the
resulting decomposition. The derivation matrix makes the progression from
foundation to tension, requirement, layer, supported property, and evaluation
construct reconstructable.

The method remains narrative and author-constructed. That is acceptable for
the declared artifact class because it is disclosed and falsifiable through
comparison with alternatives. It is not represented as a systematic review,
formal proof, or validated design theory.

## Architecture Coherence

The interface table materially strengthens the use of `architecture`. Each
layer now has a responsibility, input, output or record, dependency, boundary,
and expressly excluded authority. This resolves the earlier ambiguity between
functional modules and cross-cutting evidence, AI, and contestability
responsibilities.

The six elements are still ontologically heterogeneous: some constrain
authority, some represent capability or context, and others are cross-cutting
record or review surfaces. The revised manuscript no longer relies on implied
homogeneity. Instead, it treats the layers as responsibility and handoff
surfaces. That is scientifically coherent within the declared conceptual
architecture framing.

## Evaluation Alignment

The evaluation plan now addresses the retained contribution through three
continuous questions:

1. RQ1 evaluates traceability and reconstruction.
2. RQ2 evaluates cross-layer coherence and specified review conditions.
3. RQ3 evaluates bounded human-AI reliance.

The measures cover authority conflict, missing evidence, provenance
visibility, dependencies, unsupported transitions, challenge-path
accessibility, reviewer disagreement, error detection, override behavior, and
rationale quality. The manuscript explicitly denies that these measures prove
legitimacy, fairness, accountability, effective contestability, correction,
reversal, or remedy.

The research agenda is therefore aligned with the integrated architecture
without requiring field deployment or claiming complete institutional
validation.

## Falsifiability

The manuscript identifies negative outcomes that would count against the
architecture: failure to improve reconstruction, increased workload without
better detection, undetected cross-layer inconsistencies, disagreement about
challenge conditions, and failure to improve calibrated reliance. It also
acknowledges that alternative decompositions may be clearer or more useful.

This is sufficient falsifiability for the present conceptual-paper stage.

## Scope Concentration

Participation Readiness is now a subordinate Section `4.7` boundary rather
than a co-equal contribution. It retains accommodation, challenge,
reviewability, exclusion, surveillance, and privacy cautions without opening a
parallel theory of competence.

The L-Level and D-Level taxonomy no longer occupies a standalone scientific
section. Prototype Status preserves only the category-error distinction among
documentation, development, production, and authority. These changes improve
focus without discarding necessary evidence boundaries.

## Limitations and Overclaim

The limitations remain substantive. They address absent implementation and
field evidence, nominal oversight, AI unreliability, exclusion, hidden
coupling, constitutional rigidity or ambiguity, architectural complexity,
synthetic-scenario limits, provenance limits, and alternative decompositions.

No claim of deployment, operational readiness, users, adoption, performance,
security, legal approval, institutional validation, or human peer review was
found. The title retains `Accountability`, but the manuscript now consistently
uses accountability as an institutional objective or supported condition,
not an achieved result. This is proportionate.
