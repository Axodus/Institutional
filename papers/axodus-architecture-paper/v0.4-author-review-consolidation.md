## Executive summary

The v0.4 manuscript improves strategic fidelity to the Axodus ecosystem by
reframing the paper around federated modular digital institutions. The four
review inputs also confirm that v0.4 is not yet ready for internal peer
review. The draft remains an author-review manuscript and requires a targeted
v0.4.1 structural patch before any new review package is prepared.

The main issues are not about broad direction. They are about architectural
layering, terminology governance, policy-constraint clarity, evidence-review
requirements, and the distinction between conceptual provenance and semantic
or integrity claims.

## Consolidated verdict

- `v0.4` is strategically closer to the Axodus design than `v0.3.2`.
- `v0.4` is not authorized as an internal peer-review package.
- `v0.3.2` remains the last completed peer-review package, but should not be
  resumed if the v0.4 direction is accepted.
- `v0.4.1` is the correct next step.
- `v0.5` is premature.
- Controlled external review, publication, submission, outreach, grants,
  fundraising use, and authority signaling remain blocked.

## Implications for v0.4.1

The next patch must be short and structural. It should not reopen the thesis,
expand the product side of the ecosystem, or broaden the paper's ambition.

The primary corrective moves are:

- demote `Tenant` to a qualified manuscript note and use `bounded local
  governance domain` as the paper-facing default;
- replace `service nuclei` in the layer model with `functional service
  modules`;
- remove modules as a horizontal layer and treat them as orthogonal bounded
  capabilities configured by local governance domains;
- add constitutional invariants or policy-constraint language for local
  state transitions;
- separate provenance reconstruction from semantic validation and record
  integrity;
- require raw evidence-package access for independent review and appeal; and
- restore stronger scope-reduction heuristics.
