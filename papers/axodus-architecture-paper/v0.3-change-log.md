# v0.3 Change Log

Change date: 2026-07-03  
Scope: internal v0.3 manuscript rewrite for author review

## Summary

Phase 1.7 rewrote the manuscript around a narrower central contribution:
traceability and accountability in human-AI-assisted digital institutions.
The rewrite reduces contribution sprawl, keeps participation readiness as a
supporting mechanism, and preserves all existing publication blockers.

## Manuscript changes

- Retitled the paper to foreground traceability and accountability in
  human-AI-assisted digital institutions.
- Updated manuscript metadata from draft v0.2 to draft v0.3.
- Rewrote the abstract to center traceability, accountability, human
  oversight, prototype-stage status, and research-agenda framing.
- Rewrote the introduction to clarify:
  - the problem being addressed;
  - the research gap;
  - the paper's central contribution;
  - the prototype-stage limitation;
  - explicit out-of-scope items.
- Rebalanced background framing toward traceability, accountability,
  contestability, calibrated reliance, and institutional review.
- Narrowed the conceptual-architecture framing so that participation
  readiness, maturity separation, and risk boundaries operate as supporting
  components rather than co-equal contributions.
- Replaced public-facing manuscript emphasis with:
  - primary term: `participation readiness mechanism`;
  - secondary descriptive term: `knowledge-gated participation`;
  - internal-only label note: `Proof of Knowledge`.
- Kept `Academy` out of manuscript-facing text.
- Reordered the evaluation plan so that:
  - `RQ1` is the primary initial study;
  - `RQ3` is the secondary initial study;
  - `RQ2`, `RQ4`, and `RQ5` are reframed as future work.
- Strengthened the limitations section to state:
  - lack of empirical validation;
  - lack of operational deployment;
  - calibrated-reliance risks;
  - contestability and appeals risks;
  - participation exclusion risks;
  - the possibility that the architecture may be too complex to govern in
    practice without substantial scope reduction.
