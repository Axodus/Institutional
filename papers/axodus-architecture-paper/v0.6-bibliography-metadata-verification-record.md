## Verification Method

For each candidate, Phase `2.4`:

1. identified the exact intended work;
2. consulted the official publisher or journal record;
3. consulted a DOI registry record where applicable;
4. corroborated identity against a trusted academic library catalog or an
   equivalent authoritative source;
5. compared title, authors, date, edition, identifiers, pagination, and work
   type;
6. recorded material disagreements;
7. avoided silently choosing between conflicting records;
8. assigned a metadata-confidence level; and
9. classified whether the source may proceed to proposition-level fit
   assessment.

AI-generated metadata was not used as evidence. Retailer pages, blogs,
unsourced bibliography aggregators, citation generators, search-result
snippets, encyclopedia summaries, and secondary reviews were not used as sole
authority.

## Authoritative Source Hierarchy

The verification applied this hierarchy:

1. official journal or publisher record;
2. DOI registration record;
3. Crossref or an equivalent authoritative metadata registry;
4. official institutional or author publication page;
5. trusted academic library catalog; and
6. indexed scholarly database for corroboration.

For books, publisher records, publisher-hosted book front matter, Crossref DOI
records where available, and WorldCat records were compared. Each source log
below records the source name, source type, stable identifier, access date,
supported metadata fields, and any disagreement.

## Confidence Classification

Phase `2.4` applies these classifications:

### High confidence

- exact work identity confirmed;
- authoritative sources agree on material fields;
- DOI or ISBN verified where applicable; and
- no unresolved material conflict.

### Medium confidence

- work identity clear;
- one or more noncritical fields uncertain;
- disagreement documented; and
- uncertainty does not prevent later proposition-level assessment.

### Low confidence

- work or edition identity ambiguous;
- authoritative sources materially disagree;
- essential metadata missing; or
- DOI or ISBN cannot be reliably associated with the intended work.

Low-confidence candidates must not advance.
