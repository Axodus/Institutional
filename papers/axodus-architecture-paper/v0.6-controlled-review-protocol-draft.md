# v0.6 Controlled Review Protocol Draft

**DRAFT - NOT AUTHORIZED FOR EXTERNAL USE**

Phase: 2.11
Status: internal protocol design only
Date: 2026-07-12
Legal sufficiency: not determined
Reviewer use authorized: no
Distribution authorized: no

## Purpose

This draft defines the minimum operating protocol that would govern a
possible future controlled external review of a fixed Axodus Architecture
Paper `v0.6` package. It is planning material, not a contract, invitation,
review request, or authorization.

## Permitted Use

If later approved, a recipient may read only the approved package and provide
feedback within the assigned academic, technical, institutional, or
methodological scope. Any permission must be purpose-specific, recipient-
specific, version-specific, and time-bounded.

## Prohibited Use

The package must not be used for public distribution, publication,
submission, fundraising, investment analysis, partner due diligence,
production decisions, legal or regulatory reliance, product validation,
security certification, endorsement, or any unapproved secondary purpose.

## Review Scope

The responsible human must record the reviewer's assigned questions, excluded
topics, expected depth, and whether comments are academic, technical,
institutional, or methodological. The reviewer receives no authority to
expand manuscript scope or represent Axodus.

## Confidentiality

Any confidentiality requirement must be reviewed for compatibility with the
content license and applicable law before use. A later approved protocol must
state what is confidential, for how long, who may access it, and what actions
are required after expiration or revocation.

No legal sufficiency is claimed by this draft.

## Redistribution

No onward transmission, copying for another person, public posting, repository
mirroring, shared-link publication, or inclusion in another review system is
permitted without a separate explicit human authorization.

## Attribution

The reviewer must select whether feedback may be attributed by name,
attributed by role only, or kept externally anonymous. Identity remains
internally traceable to the responsible human. No name, affiliation, or
comment may be quoted without explicit consent.

## Retention

The approved protocol must define the review period, permitted local copies,
backup handling, feedback retention, identity-record retention, and required
disposal or continued retention after the review. Default indefinite
retention is not authorized by this draft.

## Deletion or Revocation Request

The future authorization must define how the responsible human may revoke
access, supersede a package, request deletion where legally and technically
appropriate, and record completion or inability to comply. Revocation does
not imply that previously disclosed information can always be recovered.

## Conflict Disclosure

Before receiving a package, a reviewer must disclose relevant financial,
investment, employment, advisory, competitor, personal, contribution, IP,
commercial, institutional, prior-access, and intended-reuse conflicts. The
responsible human decides eligibility, limitation, restricted scope, recusal,
or ineligibility.

## Communication Channel

The responsible human must approve a recipient-specific communication and
delivery channel. Public links, anonymous shared folders, unrestricted
repository access, and channels that cannot preserve the required audit trail
are prohibited.

## Feedback Format

Feedback must identify the package version, assigned scope, manuscript section
or proposition, severity, description, evidence or rationale, and recommended
action. Allowed classifications are critical, major, minor, advisory, and out
of scope.

## Deadline

The review invitation, if later authorized, must specify a start date,
feedback deadline, access-expiration date where available, and the handling of
late feedback. No deadline is selected in Phase `2.11`.

## Escalation

Suspected leakage, unauthorized redistribution, scope expansion, conflict,
misrepresentation, or security exposure must be reported to the responsible
human. The response may suspend access, revoke authorization, preserve
evidence, and require a separate incident decision.

## Data Handling

Collect only the reviewer identity, declarations, communication, access
records, feedback, consent choices, and decision evidence required for the
review. Access must be role-limited and retention must follow an approved
privacy decision.

## Version Control

The reviewer may receive only the exact artifacts listed in an approved
manifest. Each artifact must have a stable filename and SHA-256. Silent file
replacement and reuse of a superseded package are prohibited.

## Feedback Decision Process

Receipt is logged; feedback is classified; evidence is requested where
needed; and the author records accept, partially accept, defer, or reject.
Any manuscript change requires a separate approved synchronized patch. Review
completion never triggers publication automatically.

## Disclaimer

The manuscript is a conceptual, prototype-stage academic draft. It reports no
deployment, empirical validation, operational readiness, legal compliance,
investment suitability, security assurance, or production authority.

## No Endorsement

Participation does not establish endorsement, partnership, certification,
validation, institutional adoption, or approval by the reviewer, the
reviewer's institution, cited authors, or any other organization.

## No Publication or Peer-Review Claim

Controlled review is not publication, open peer review, journal review,
conference review, acceptance, or public release. Neither the author nor the
reviewer may characterize the manuscript as peer reviewed solely because this
protocol was used.

## No Reliance

The package must not be relied upon for legal, financial, investment,
regulatory, security, product, deployment, or production decisions.

## Responsible Human

A future authorization must identify the human responsible for package
approval, reviewer approval, disclosure scope, transmission, incident
handling, feedback disposition, and closure. No model, agent, or automated
workflow may assume this role.

## Acceptance and Evidence

If later approved for use, protocol acceptance must be recorded before any
package transmission. The record must identify recipient, protocol version,
package ID, date, scope, conflicts, attribution choice, and retention terms.

## Human and Legal Review Required

This draft requires explicit human review and qualified legal review where
appropriate, especially for license compatibility, confidentiality,
redistribution, privacy, retention, jurisdiction, liability, and feedback
rights. Phase `2.11` grants no approval.
