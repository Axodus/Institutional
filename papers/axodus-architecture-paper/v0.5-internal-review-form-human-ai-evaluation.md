## Overall assessment

In 150–300 words, summarize the paper's human--AI framing, strongest
evaluation-design feature, principal bounded-assistance risk, and most
important revision:

Response:
The manuscript now frames human-AI assistance in a disciplined way: AI is
treated as attributable, bounded, and review-supporting rather than as an
autonomous institutional actor. Its strongest evaluation-design feature is the
decision to keep RQ1 and RQ3 inside synthetic, controlled, review-oriented
study designs rather than implying an existing Axodus platform or completed
experiment. The principal remaining bounded-assistance risk is methodological
rather than conceptual. Section 8 names a document-only baseline, independent
reviewers, and several candidate study criteria, but it still leaves the
minimum baseline assumptions and reviewer-role conditions underspecified for a
later protocol-drafting phase. That does not make the current paper invalid,
but it does mean a later empirical-planning step should define those terms
more tightly without importing any operating-platform implication. The most
important revision is therefore a narrow future evaluation cleanup, not a
larger change to the human-AI thesis.
