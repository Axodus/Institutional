## Issue Being Resolved

An internal reviewer identified a version-traceability concern: a public
branch view may appear to show an older paper state while the active `v0.5`
internal review package refers to a newer internal draft.

This concern affects:

- review traceability;
- internal review validity;
- external auditability; and
- later readiness decisions.

a later phase.

However, no readiness advancement, no external-use decision, and no claim of
externally auditable manuscript-state continuity may occur until the
working-version location issue is resolved explicitly.

## Required Future Correction

Before any future review cycle, readiness transition, or external-use
decision:

1. record a `current working version location` field in the relevant

2. identify the reviewed manuscript by branch, commit, package hash, or

3. state whether `main` is current, intentionally behind, frozen, or pending

4. ensure that reviewers can distinguish:
   - the active internal review artifact;
   - the local repository state; and
   - any external/public branch view.

If branch-location certainty is unavailable, the package hash lock remains the
minimum acceptable identifier for internal review, and external circulation
must remain blocked.
