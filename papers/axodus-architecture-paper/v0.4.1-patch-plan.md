# v0.4.1 Patch Plan

Historical note: this file is retained for traceability only. Its forward
path was canceled by the accepted Phase 1.20 transition to v0.5.

Status: historical superseded Phase 1.19 authorization record
Date: 2026-07-04  
Patch opened: no  
Manuscript editing authorized: no; superseded by the active v0.5 track

## Objective

Correct the structural, terminology, and policy-layer issues identified in
author review while preserving the v0.4 thesis and all publication blockers.

## Authorized patch scope

The v0.4.1 patch is authorized to do the following:

1. Replace paper-primary uses of `Tenant` with `bounded local governance
   domain`.
2. Add one concise conceptual note stating that Axodus institutional
   documentation refers to such domains as `Tenants`, without implying SaaS
   tenancy, customers, or deployed communities.
3. Replace `service nuclei` as a manuscript layer term with `functional
   service modules`.
4. Rework the architecture so functional service modules are orthogonal
   bounded capabilities configured by local governance domains rather than a
   horizontal layer.
5. Add constitutional invariants or policy-constraint language that clarifies
   which local transitions must be validated against central rules.
6. Strengthen the distinction among provenance reconstruction, semantic
   validation, and record integrity.
7. State that independent review and appeal require access to the raw
   evidence package, not only derived summaries or rationale records.
8. Remove any residual `Web3-facing` wording or equivalent domain-first
   framing.
9. Restore stronger scope-reduction heuristics so complexity control regains
   architectural weight.

## Explicitly not authorized

The v0.4.1 patch must not:

- reopen the thesis;
- introduce a product catalog in the paper body;
- add tokenomics, `$Neurons`, or Academy distribution mechanics;
- add named agents;
- add regulated financial-module detail;
- add operational, user, adoption, revenue, treasury, production, security,
  partner, funder, pilot, or institutional-validation claims;
- add new verified references unless they are separately verified first; or
- open v0.5.

## Expected manuscript effects

The v0.4.1 patch should leave the paper:

- closer to the Axodus design than v0.3.2;
- more precise about governance layering than v0.4;
- less ambiguous about module terminology;
- clearer about what provenance does and does not establish; and
- still firmly within prototype-stage and non-operational boundaries.

## Readiness target after patch

The target after v0.4.1 is not peer review and not publication. The target
is a cleaner internal draft and a fresh readiness package that can decide
whether a new internal peer-review gate should open.
