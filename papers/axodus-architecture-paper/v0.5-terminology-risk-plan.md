## Current Terminology Risk Summary

### 4.2 Tenant governance layer

Current manuscript risk:

- `Tenant` can be read as deployed SaaS tenancy, active customer entity,
  operating community, or production infrastructure.
- `Tenant governance layer` can be read as an implemented multi-tenant
  governance environment rather than a conceptual bounded governance domain.

### 4.3 Service nuclei layer

Current manuscript risk:

- `service nuclei` can be read as a product catalog, internal module
  inventory, institutional portfolio disclosure, or implemented system
  decomposition.
- `Service nuclei layer` can be read as a horizontal implementation layer
  rather than a paper-facing abstraction for bounded institutional
  capabilities.

## Approved Manuscript-Facing Terminology Decisions

### Section 4.2

Future paper-facing heading:

- `Bounded local governance domain layer`

Future paper-facing terminology:

- replace `Tenant` / `Tenants` as the primary manuscript terms with
  `bounded local governance domain` /
  `bounded local governance domains`

Allowed clarification sentence, at most once:

> Axodus institutional documentation may use the term Tenant for such bounded
> local governance domains; this paper uses the governance-domain phrasing to
> avoid implying deployed SaaS tenancy, active customer entities, operating
> communities, or production infrastructure.

### Section 4.3

Future paper-facing heading:

- `Functional service module layer`

Future paper-facing terminology:

- replace `service nucleus` / `service nuclei` as the primary manuscript
  terms with `functional service module` /
  `functional service modules`

Allowed clarification sentence, at most once:

> Axodus institutional documentation may use the term service nuclei in
> broader institutional contexts; this paper uses functional service modules
> as the architecture-facing abstraction to avoid implying a product catalog,
> implementation inventory, or deployed system decomposition.

## Allowed Replacements

The future terminology patch may:

  cataloging, inventory disclosure, or operational system decomposition; and
- update `paper.md` and `paper.tex` in parallel for synchronization only.

## One-Note Maximum Policy

Institutional terminology may survive in the manuscript only under the
following rule:

- `Tenant` may appear at most once as a bounded clarification note inside
  Section `4.2`.
- `service nuclei` may appear at most once as a bounded clarification note
  inside Section `4.3`.
- Neither term should remain the primary manuscript-facing heading or default
  prose term.
