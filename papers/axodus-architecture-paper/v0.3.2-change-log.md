# v0.3.2 Targeted Academic Hardening Change Log

Date: 2026-07-03  
Scope: Phase 1.15 targeted internal revision  
Result: Ready for internal peer review after targeted revision

## Purpose

The v0.3.2 patch implements the bounded academic-hardening scope authorized by
the Phase 1.14 internal review consolidation. It strengthens architecture and
evaluation detail without opening v0.4 or changing the prototype-stage
evidence status.

## Manuscript changes

### Contribution and abstract

- Updated the version and internal-review status to v0.3.2.
- Rebalanced the abstract by replacing the L/D maturity reference with
  complexity reduction.
- Clarified that the contribution is the proposed arrangement of authority,
  evidence, attributable AI assistance, rationale, contestability, and review,
  not any layer in isolation.

### Architectural flow

- Added a six-step conceptual decision path from constitutional authority
  through evidence, assistance, risk constraints, human disposition, and
  independent review or appeal.
- Distinguished information flow, control flow, and review flow without
  describing implementation topology.
- Added one explicitly synthetic, non-empirical policy-exception scenario.

### Provenance

- Added a related-work bridge to W3C PROV-DM concepts: entities, activities,
  agents, derivation, attribution, and responsibility relationships.
- Added a conceptual mapping of source artifacts, analysis or review
  activities, and responsible human or automated participants.
- Stated that provenance vocabulary does not prescribe storage, integrity, or
  accountability outcomes.

### DeFi and broader coupling

- Reduced Schär/DeFi to one domain-specific example in related work.
- Removed the repeated DeFi dependency example from the risk section.
- Added a publication-blocking literature marker for complex-system coupling
  and socio-technical failure.

### Participation readiness and contestability

- Added proposed criteria for exclusion, accessibility, appeals, curriculum
  concentration, privacy burden, and context-specific usefulness.
- Added candidate safeguards around curriculum/authorization separation,
  conflict disclosure, and independent review.
- Expanded contestability to cover reviewer independence, recusal,
  escalation, capacity, review time, secondary review, role rotation,
  multi-role review, and limits of nominal oversight.
- Added a publication-blocking literature marker for contestability and
  institutional appeals.

### Complexity and evaluation

- Retitled Section 5 to include scope reduction.
- Added heuristics for minimum decision records, advisory/authoritative
  separation, evidence packages, proportional review, and component removal.
- Added proposed RQ1 and RQ3 measures without reporting results.
- Added a staged future path from artifact inspection through synthetic review
  studies and separately gated prototype or institutionally situated work.
- Strengthened limitations around synthetic scenarios, provenance vocabulary,
  proposed measures, and field effects.
