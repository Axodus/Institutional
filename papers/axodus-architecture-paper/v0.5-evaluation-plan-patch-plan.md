## Current Role of Section 8

Section `8` currently presents a prioritized evaluation proposal centered on:

- a controlled synthetic environment;
- RQ1 traceability and reconstruction;
- RQ3 human-AI reliance in advisory review; and
- staged future evaluation under separately approved protocols.

Its current role is to describe study design intent, not to report evidence,
results, platform capability, or completed evaluation.

## Planned Modification Type

- controlled evaluation-framing refinement

## Allowed Intervention

Future Section `8` work may only:

- preserve evaluation as proposed, synthetic, conceptual, or future work;
- tighten language so study criteria remain clearly non-empirical and
  non-reported;
- clarify that the environment is a controlled mock or prototype-study setup,
  not an active platform;
- clarify that proposed measures are study criteria, not observed metrics;
- preserve the staged-gating logic for any future institutionally situated
  study;
- keep RQ1 and RQ3 as study-design priorities without implying execution; and
- align Section `8` with the manuscript's prototype-stage and
  non-operational boundaries.

## Forbidden Intervention

Future Section `8` work must not introduce:

- real metrics presented as existing evidence;
- completed-study language;
- empirical results;
- user counts;
- adoption claims;
- active-platform language;
- deployment claims;
- partner or institution claims;
- product demonstration claims;
- real reviewer population claims;
- tokenomics or financial framing;
- new citations unless separately approved; or
- bibliography edits in this phase line.

## Claim-Boundary Risks

The future Section `8` patch must treat the following as live risks:

- proposed measures can sound like reported metrics;
- synthetic scenarios can sound like completed evaluation;
- mock or prototype-study language can drift into active platform claims;
- reviewer-study wording can sound like current user or adoption evidence;
- staged future evaluation can sound like an approved operational roadmap; and
- broadening the section can imply empirical maturity that the paper does not
  have.

## Proposed Future Patch Shape

The future Section `8` patch should:

- keep the section compact and prioritized;
- avoid new subsections unless strictly necessary;
- avoid new citations;
- preserve RQ1 and RQ3 as proposal-stage study priorities;
- state clearly that all measures are proposed study criteria;
- keep synthetic scenarios and mock-up language explicitly non-operational;
- preserve separately gated language for any later institutionally situated
  study; and
- avoid any claim that evaluation has begun, completed, or produced evidence.
