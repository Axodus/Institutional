# Paper Scope Impact Analysis

Status: Phase 1.16A author-alignment analysis  
Manuscript affected: v0.3.2  
Manuscript edits authorized: no

## Executive assessment

The author clarification is a premise-level change, not an incremental review
finding. The current manuscript asks how traceability and accountability can
support generic human--AI-assisted digital institutions. The corrected Axodus
model instead requires the paper to explain accountability across a
constitutional federation, delegated Tenant authority, optional service
nuclei, and bounded AI assistance.

The v0.3.2 contribution remains reusable, but its institutional object is too
generic. A targeted v0.3.3 patch would require changes to the title, abstract,
problem statement, architecture, terminology, evaluation, and limitations.
That scope exceeds a targeted patch.

## Safe academic framing

> Axodus is proposed as a prototype-stage conceptual architecture for
> traceability and accountability in federated modular digital institutions,
> where constitutionally constrained Tenants may select and govern service
> nuclei through human--AI-assisted decision processes.

The architecture is motivated by a federated modular ecosystem in which
constitutionally constrained Tenant entities may configure and govern service
nuclei through traceable, reviewable, human--AI-assisted decisions. A future
paper should abstract away from the specific product inventory, token design,
financial operation, and implementation repositories.

## Candidate future architecture

A future reframing should distinguish:

1. a central constitutional layer defining non-delegable constraints;
2. Tenant governance domains exercising bounded local authority;
3. optional service nuclei governed through explicit module contracts;
4. shared evidence and provenance requirements;
5. abstract constitutional-analysis, adversarial-assurance, and operational-
   planning assistance roles; and
6. human review, escalation, appeal, and risk boundaries.

Each module contract should identify purpose, Tenant opt-in rules, delegated
authority, configurable parameters, evidence inputs and outputs, dependencies,
escalation conditions, failure behavior, and review paths. This is a
conceptual interface proposal, not an implementation inventory.

## Evaluation implications

Future synthetic studies should test whether reviewers can reconstruct the
authority chain from the constitutional layer through a Tenant decision and
module configuration; detect conflicts between central constraints and local
decisions; distinguish advisory AI output from authoritative human
disposition; and identify when modular complexity requires escalation or
scope reduction.

Illustrative scenarios must remain synthetic and should avoid regulated
financial modules unless a specific legal and methodological review authorizes
their inclusion.

## Scope decision

Internal peer review of v0.3.2 should remain paused. The next authorized action
is author review of a v0.4 reframing proposal, not manuscript editing.
