## Assessment Method

For each active candidate, Phase `2.5`:

1. identified an exact proposition already present in the manuscript;
2. identified its section, subsection, and stable paragraph anchor;
3. summarized the target proposition narrowly;
4. classified source support as direct, indirect, contextual, or absent;
5. classified the proposed citation function as foundational, descriptive,
   comparative, critical, or contextual;
6. distinguished existing manuscript content from any new
   literature-positioning sentence;
7. assessed whether wording changes are required;
8. assessed claim-expansion risk;
9. assessed operational, Academy, governance, financial, legal, and
   private-track boundary risk; and
10. issued an `accept for future patch`, `accept conditionally`, `defer`, or
    `reject` decision.

Weak, prestige-based, or merely thematic fit is insufficient.

## Ostrom Proposition-Level Assessment

### Exact existing proposition

Section `4.2`, paragraph beginning `The bounded local governance domain layer
represents...`, states that a locally scoped decision context operates under
constitutional constraints and configures processes and modules only within a
permitted scope.

### Source-support assessment

Ostrom's article directly describes polycentric systems as involving multiple
decision centers that may be formally distinct yet interdependent. It also
describes governance activities organized across nested layers. These points
support a bounded conceptual reference for distributed but coordinated local
governance domains.

The source does not support the Axodus-specific constitutional layer,
non-delegable constraints, escalation process, or implementation. The citation
must therefore attach to a separate literature-positioning sentence and not to
the full Axodus design paragraph as if it were validation.

- Exact target: Section `4.2`, first paragraph.
- Support directness: direct for the proposed polycentric-reference sentence;
  contextual for the existing Axodus design.
- Citation function: contextual and foundational.
- Section `2.1` citation required: no.
- Section `4.2` citation required: yes, one location only.
- Wording adjustment required: yes, one prefatory sentence.
- Existing-source duplication: none. Baxter and Sommerville support the
  socio-technical framing, not polycentric governance.
- Claim-expansion risk: low if the wording remains comparative and bounded.
- Boundary risk: low if implementation equivalence and operational governance
  claims remain excluded.

### Minimal wording direction

Prepend this sentence to the first paragraph of Section `4.2`:

> As a conceptual reference rather than an implementation equivalence,
> polycentric governance describes multiple decision centers that may be
> formally distinct yet interdependent and may operate across nested
> governance layers [@ostrom2010beyond].

The LaTeX version must use the same wording and replace the Markdown citation
with `\citep{ostrom2010beyond}`.

### Decision

**Accept for future patch.**

This decision approves only the fit and patch direction recorded here. It does
not approve bibliography inclusion, manuscript editing, or external use.

## Baldwin and Clark Proposition-Level Assessment

### Exact existing proposition

Section `4.3`, paragraph beginning `Functional service modules are abstract
bounded institutional capabilities...`, treats a module as a conceptual unit
with a defined purpose, inputs, outputs, scope, dependencies, and interaction
conditions.

### Source-support assessment

Baldwin and Clark directly support the general modularity proposition that a
complex system may be composed of smaller subsystems that can be designed
separately while functioning together under shared design rules. This provides
a bounded conceptual basis for functional separation without implying
institutional autonomy.

The source does not validate the Axodus six-layer architecture, the proposed
functional taxonomy, deployment success, or the normative institutional
checklist in Section `4.3`. The citation must attach to a separate
literature-positioning sentence.

- Exact target: Section `4.3`, first paragraph.
- Support directness: direct for the proposed modularity-reference sentence;
  contextual for the existing Axodus module definition.
- Citation function: foundational and contextual.
- Section `2.2` citation required: no.
- Section `4.3` citation required: yes, one location only.
- Wording adjustment required: yes, one prefatory sentence.
- Existing-source duplication: none. Baldwin and Clark support modular
  separation and shared design rules; Leveson supports system safety,
  interaction failures, control mismatches, and failure propagation.
- Claim-expansion risk: low if no optimality, validation, or implementation
  claim is added.
- Boundary risk: low if functional modules are not assigned autonomous
  institutional authority.

### Minimal wording direction

Prepend this sentence to the first paragraph of Section `4.3`:

> As a conceptual reference rather than validation of this taxonomy,
> modularity literature describes complex systems built from smaller
> subsystems that can be designed separately while functioning together under
> shared design rules [@baldwin2000design].

The LaTeX version must use the same wording and replace the Markdown citation
with `\citep{baldwin2000design}`.

### Leveson distinction

The existing Leveson citation remains unchanged in Section `2.2`. Leveson
supports the safety proposition that failures may arise from interactions,
control mismatches, and coupling. Baldwin and Clark support the modular-design
proposition concerning separable subsystems and shared design rules. Neither
source may be used for the other's proposition.

### Decision

**Accept for future patch.**

This decision approves only the fit and patch direction recorded here. It does
not approve bibliography inclusion, manuscript editing, or external use.
