## Required Planned Corrections

### A. CON-02 — Front Matter/Status Alignment

Affected:

- `paper.md` front matter/status
- `paper.tex` equivalent metadata/status

Plan:

- align manuscript front matter/status with current `v0.5 internal draft`
  state;
- preserve draft, non-publication, no-external-circulation, and
  no-publication blockers;
- do not imply publication readiness; and
- do not alter author/contact metadata unless required by status consistency.

Minimal direction:

- current manuscript state: `v0.5 internal draft`
- not peer reviewed externally
- not publication ready
- not externally circulated

### B. CON-03 and CON-04 — Section 4.3 Resynchronization and Boundary Clarification

Affected:

- Section `4.3` in `paper.md` and `paper.tex`

Plan:

- resynchronize Section `4.3` across Markdown and LaTeX;
- clarify that functional service modules are bounded functional design
  surfaces;
- clarify that they do not absorb, replace, or duplicate:
  - evidence/provenance layer;
  - AI-assistance layer;
  - risk-boundary and contestability layer; or
  - constitutional constraints;
- preserve non-product, non-inventory, non-implementation framing; and
- keep the institutional-terminology note bounded.

Minimal direction:

- functional service modules expose domain-specific capability surfaces only
  within constitutional and local-governance constraints;
- recordkeeping, AI support, and contestability remain separate cross-cutting
  design layers, not module-owned authority.

### C. CON-05 — Constitutional/Local Conflict Rule

Affected:

- Sections `4.1` and/or `4.2`

Plan:

- add a conceptual rule that bounded local governance domains cannot expand or
  override non-delegable constitutional constraints;
- ambiguous conflicts should trigger review, documentation, or escalation
  conditions before final disposition; and
- keep language non-operational and non-legal.

Minimal direction:

- local discretion is bounded by constitutional constraints;
- when boundary interpretation is disputed, the architecture treats the
  conflict as a review condition, not as automatic local authority.

### D. CON-06 — Constitutional Reviewability

Affected:

- Section `4.1`

Plan:

- clarify that constitutional constraints themselves require reviewability,
  amendment paths, and contestability;
- avoid suggesting an active amendment process, legal constitution, or real
  governance body.

Minimal direction:

- constitutional constraints are not assumed infallible;
- they must remain inspectable, revisable, and challengeable as design
  objects.

### E. CON-07 — Delegated Authority Wording

Affected:

- Section `4.3`

Verification result in this phase:

- `delegated authority` is present in the current manuscript state in both
  `paper.md` and `paper.tex`.

Plan:

- replace with `authorized scope and decision dependencies` or equivalent
  boundary-safe wording;
- if a later review-copy no longer contains the phrase, record no action.

### F. CON-08 — Minimal Contestability Fields

Affected:

- Section `4.6`

Plan:

- add a compact conceptual description of the minimum fields needed for
  challengeability:
  - contested decision or record;
  - applicable rule or boundary;
  - disputed evidence or rationale;
  - review-independence condition;
  - possible disposition category such as correction, escalation condition,
    refusal, reversal, or no change;
- avoid `appeals process`, `moderation`, `enforcement`, `binding review`,
  `review board`, `compliance process`, or live dispute-resolution language.

Minimal direction:

- these are conceptual fields for reconstructability and challengeability, not
  an operating procedure.

### G. CON-09 — Evaluation Baseline and Negative Findings

Affected:

- Section `8`

Plan:

- define `document-only baseline` as a comparison condition involving static
  policy or record documentation without the proposed layered review
  architecture;
- add examples of possible negative findings:
  - reviewers fail to reconstruct decisions better than the baseline;
  - provenance increases workload without improving error detection;
  - challenge paths are inconsistently classified;
  - simulated AI records do not improve calibrated reliance;
- keep all language proposed, synthetic, and conceptual; and
- do not imply a completed study or real metrics.

### H. CON-10 — Residual Appeal Wording

Affected:

- manuscript-wide only if confined and safe

Verification result in this phase:

- residual `appeal` / `appeals` wording is present in the current local
  manuscript state in both Markdown and LaTeX.

Plan:

- replace only where it is not part of quoted, historical, or analytically
  necessary text and where `challenge path`, `review condition`, or
  `contestability` is safer;
- if a specific instance remains necessary, keep it bounded and explicitly
  conceptual.
