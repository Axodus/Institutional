# Institutional Public Disclosure Boundary

Status: Phase 1.16A controlled public disclosure policy

## Purpose

The Axodus Institutional repository is intentionally public. It may describe
the ecosystem's institutional architecture, governance model, research
direction, modular structure, and public-facing strategic rationale. This
authorization does not make the private product repositories, implementations,
or operational environments public.

Public availability is not evidence that a document is publication-ready,
that a proposed system exists operationally, or that any module has users,
adoption, revenue, validated security, or production authority.

## Permitted public institutional disclosure

Subject to the evidence and review rules in this repository, Institutional
documentation may include:

- the Axodus institutional vision and prototype-stage conceptual architecture;
- constitutional and Tenant-level governance concepts;
- the names and bounded conceptual functions of product or service nuclei;
- conceptual relationships, dependencies, and authority boundaries among
  nuclei;
- author-reported broad planning labels such as `planned`, `prototype`, or
  `under development`, when explicitly qualified as unverified and not
  indicative of readiness;
- research foundations, hypotheses, evaluation proposals, and documented
  editorial decisions; and
- publication-readiness and disclosure records.

Institutional documentation may be read by prospective technical partners,
analysts, reviewers, or investors. That audience does not authorize directed
outreach, fundraising language, solicitation, investment claims, promises of
return, or promotional overstatement.

## Controlled module disclosure

The module inventory may be presented as an author-approved institutional view
of the intended ecosystem. A module name or conceptual description does not
establish that the module is implemented, integrated, secure, adopted,
operational, revenue-generating, or ready for production.

Regulated or financially sensitive domains, including decentralized finance,
exchange, trading, mining, lottery, treasury, staking, token distribution, and
investment-related activity, require narrower language and separate legal,
regulatory, economic, and evidence review. Public institutional descriptions
must not disclose strategies, parameters, performance, returns, capital
positions, counterparties, or execution details.

Public governance-mechanics documents may describe governance structure,
checks and balances, review paths, and unresolved procedural concepts in
abstract form. They must not disclose tokenomics, issuance mechanics,
valuation hypotheses, price targets, liquidity strategy, or other sensitive
economic design without a separate approved disclosure path.

## Prohibited disclosure

The public Institutional repository must not expose:

- source code or proprietary implementation logic from private nuclei;
- credentials, keys, secrets, tokens, private endpoints, or access details;
- private repository names, paths, links, or infrastructure arrangements;
- deployment topology, exploitable architecture, security controls, attack
  detail, or sensitive defensive procedures;
- implementation-specific prompts, orchestration rules, thresholds, or
  autonomous execution controls;
- user, partner, counterparty, personnel, or other personal or confidential
  data;
- internal financial data, treasury positions, trading strategies, mining
  economics, token mechanics, or non-public commercial terms; or
- unreviewed claims about production, adoption, users, revenue, performance,
  safety, security, regulatory compliance, or comparative superiority.

## Document-specific boundaries

The academic architecture paper should abstract the module inventory and focus
on the federated modular governance model, traceability, accountability, and
human oversight. A future institutional overview or whitepaper may describe
the portfolio in greater detail only after its own evidence, legal, IP, and
publication review. Token design and distribution require separate tokenomics
and legal review. Technical implementation remains in private technical
documentation.

## Review rule

Every proposed disclosure must be assessed separately for:

1. evidence strength;
2. operational and maturity implications;
3. intellectual-property and security exposure;
4. financial or regulatory sensitivity; and
5. suitability for the target document.

When a public conceptual description would reveal sensitive implementation or
create an unsupported claim, replace it with an interface-level abstraction
and record the omission or blocker in the relevant readiness report.
