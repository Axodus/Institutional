# Citation Policy

Status: Phase 1 editorial control

## Source classes

1. **Verified and cited**: metadata checked against a primary record and used
   in the manuscript. These sources belong in `references.bib`.
2. **Verified but not cited**: metadata checked, relevant to the research
   program, and recorded in `research/bibliography-verified.md`.
3. **Candidate**: potentially relevant but not fully checked. Candidates are
   not citable and remain in `research/bibliography-candidates.md`.
4. **Institutional context**: author-provided information about Axodus. It is
   governed by the claims register and is not equivalent to public evidence.

## Verification requirements

Check title, complete author list or responsible institution, year, venue,
volume/issue/pages where applicable, DOI or canonical URL, and document type.
Prefer the publisher, journal, standards body, institutional repository, or
canonical preprint page.

Do not use search-result snippets, generated summaries, aggregators, or
secondary citations as the sole metadata source.

## Manuscript rules

- Every citation key must resolve to one `references.bib` entry.
- Every BibTeX entry must be cited or intentionally documented as retained.
- Claims requiring support but lacking it use `[REFERENCE NEEDED]`.
- A marker is a publication blocker, not a substitute for a citation.
- Paraphrase accurately and avoid unnecessary quotation.
- Do not cite a source as evidence for a proposition it does not support.

## Internal claims

Internal statements use the claims register, not academic citations. They may
enter a manuscript only when the evidence-boundary policy permits them and the
wording matches the approved form.
