governance model at a more concrete level than the academic architecture

the Institutional repository.

## Current governance direction

Axodus is proposed as a constitutionally centered ecosystem with bounded local
governance domains operating under higher-order rules. The governance model is
intended to combine:

- a central constitutional authority layer;
- a stakeholder proposal and refinement layer;
- a community ratification and contestation layer;
- a rapid risk-response and procedural review function; and
- bounded local governance domains that configure institutional capabilities
  within central constraints.

The model is still prototype-stage. Parameters and exact procedures remain
subject to future governance definition.

## Conceptual functions

### Constitutional center

Defines higher-order rules, role boundaries, prohibitions, amendment paths,
review triggers, and non-delegable constraints. This layer exists to preserve
governability and coherence across local domains and bounded institutional
capabilities.

### Stakeholder proposal and refinement layer

Supports proposal intake, structured refinement, review preparation,
challenge surfacing, and procedural clarification before broader
ratification or escalation.

### Community ratification and contestation layer

Supports broader visibility, bounded participation, ratification, challenge,
and contestation within whatever future governance conditions are formally
approved.

### Rapid risk-response and procedural review function

Supports urgent review, exception handling, procedural stabilization, and
escalation when a proposed action appears to conflict with constitutional
constraints, evidence requirements, or risk boundaries.

### Bounded local governance domains

Locally scoped governance domains may configure bounded institutional
capabilities under central constitutional constraints. They are conceptual
governance units, not evidence of deployed SaaS tenancy, customers, or
operating communities.
