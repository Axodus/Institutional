# Research Gaps

Status: preliminary problem framing, not empirical findings

## 1. Fragmented digital operations

Complex digital initiatives often distribute governance, documentation,
education, risk management, and operations across tools and teams. The
research question is whether a shared institutional model can make their
dependencies visible without creating a monolithic control layer.

Existing platform and workflow approaches coordinate tasks, but they do not
necessarily encode decision rights, evidence requirements, or accountability.
Axodus proposes modular institutional components linked by common governance
controls. It remains necessary to validate whether this reduces fragmentation
or merely adds another coordination layer.

## 2. Governance in Web3 ecosystems

On-chain execution does not by itself settle who frames proposals, interprets
rules, handles exceptions, or bears responsibility. Research on DAOs also
distinguishes technical decentralization from organizational governance.

Axodus proposes explicit constitutional rules, human review, and auditable
decision records around modular operations. The model has not been tested
against real governance disputes, capture, apathy, or emergency conditions.

## 3. Accountability in decentralized systems

Distributed authority can obscure causal and remedial responsibility. Public
records may improve traceability while still failing to identify who was
authorized, informed, or accountable for a decision.

The proposed model associates roles, proposals, evidence, decisions, and
review paths. Future work must determine whether those records are complete,
usable, contestable, and privacy-preserving.

## 4. Education-linked participation

Financial or token-weighted access does not establish that a participant
understands a proposal. Conversely, education gates can exclude legitimate
participants, encode cultural bias, and consolidate credentialing power.

Proof of Knowledge is proposed as an educational readiness mechanism, not as
a guarantee of competence or good judgment. Its validity, accessibility,
appeal process, privacy properties, and effect on participation require
empirical study.

## 5. Human–AI coordination

AI systems can summarize, compare, monitor, and draft, but those capabilities
can also create automation bias, unclear responsibility, and unverifiable
recommendations.

Axodus proposes AI assistance bounded by human decision authority, provenance,
and escalation. The design requires task-level evaluation of accuracy,
calibration, auditability, and appropriate reliance.

## 6. Public maturity representation

Roadmaps and status labels often combine documentation quality, implementation
progress, validation, and deployment into one ambiguous measure.

Axodus proposes separate L0–L5 documentation and D0–D5 development axes. This
draft does not define their levels. Future work must define observable
criteria, inter-rater reliability, evidence requirements, and resistance to
status inflation.

## 7. Risk and treasury transparency

Visible transactions do not necessarily make risk assumptions, authorization,
conflicts, or decision rationales understandable.

The architecture proposes policy-bound decisions and auditable records. It
does not claim a working treasury, effective limits, or financial performance.
Validation requires simulations before any live-value setting.

## 8. Documentation-to-operation transition

Detailed documentation may remain disconnected from executable procedures,
actual authority, and observed behavior.

Axodus treats documentation maturity and development maturity as separate
dimensions. Research must test whether traceable transitions between them
improve delivery without turning provisional design documents into premature
operational claims.
